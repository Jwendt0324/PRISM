## 1. Purpose

LDT is the [Methodology Partner] model for skill development. Every team member and apprentice progresses through Learn, Do, Teach for each skill they acquire. The 9 Triangles canon defines LDT as a triangle but does not explain HOW to implement it. This document fills that gap.

A team lead should be able to use this guide to design a training plan for any new skill — from article writing to Dollar-a-Day campaign management to client onboarding.

LDT is not optional. It is how competency is built and verified. Skipping phases is the single most common reason people claim expertise they do not have. [Your Mentor/Advisor]'s rule: "You can't teach what you haven't done. You can't do what you haven't learned."

## 2. The Three Phases

### LEARN Phase

**Format:** Watch or read existing content — courses, SOPs, recorded sessions, walkthroughs, and canon documents.

**Duration:** Varies by skill complexity.
- Simple tasks (e.g., EOD report submission, Basecamp navigation): 1-2 hours
- Moderate tasks (e.g., article writing from transcripts, MAA reporting): 1-2 days
- Complex workflows (e.g., full Content Factory pipeline, Knowledge Panel Sprint): 1-2 weeks

**Completion Criteria:**
- Can explain the process in their own words (not recite it)
- Can identify the key quality checks for the skill
- Passes a comprehension quiz or verbal walkthrough with their manager
- Can articulate WHY each step exists, not just WHAT the step is

**AI Role:** AI can create study guides, quizzes, summaries, and flashcards from existing SOPs and course materials. Human must verify understanding — AI cannot assess whether someone truly grasps a concept vs. has memorized an answer.

**Output:** A written summary of what they learned. This is not a formality. Writing forces comprehension. If they cannot write a clear summary, they have not learned it.

**Example:** A new Content Specialist assigned to learn article writing would read the Article Writing from Transcripts SOP, the Article QA 18-Step Checklist, and the Article Guidelines canon doc. Their Learn output: a 1-page summary explaining the process, the quality gates, and the most common failure modes.

---

### DO Phase

**Format:** Execute the skill with supervision.

**Minimum Reps:** 3 successful executions before advancing. This is the [Methodology Partner] standard — proven through the checklist validation rule: "Proven by at least 3 people executing successfully 3 times each."

**Supervision Model:**
- **First attempt:** Reviewed in detail by the supervisor. Line-by-line feedback. Every error is a teaching moment.
- **Second attempt:** Spot-checked. Supervisor reviews key quality gates but does not audit every line.
- **Third attempt:** Reviewed for consistency. Does the output match the quality of the first two? Can they sustain it without heavy guidance?

**Completion Criteria:** 3 consecutive executions that pass QA without major revisions. Minor revisions (formatting, small edits) are acceptable. Major revisions (structural problems, factual errors, AI language detection, missed quality gates) reset the count.

**AI Role:** AI can assist execution — drafting, formatting, research, data pulling. Human must review output quality. The DO phase proves the person can direct AI effectively and catch its mistakes, not that AI can do the work for them.

**Output:** A portfolio of 3+ completed work samples that passed QA. These become reference examples for future learners.

**Example:** [Your Content Specialist] writing his first 3 articles from transcripts. [Your Name] and [Your Mentor/Advisor] reviewed article 1 in detail (caught AI language). Article 2 was spot-checked. Article 3 was reviewed for consistency against the first two. Only after 3 clean articles did he move to independent production.

---

### TEACH Phase

**Format:** Create teaching content about the skill.

**Options (choose at least one):**
- Write or improve an SOP
- Record a walkthrough video (screen share + narration)
- Create a checklist that someone else can follow
- Mentor a new team member through their Learn and Do phases
- Build a MICRO course module

**Completion Criteria:** Their teaching material is good enough that someone else can Learn from it. The test: hand their output to a new person. If that person can reach the DO phase using only the teaching material, the Teach phase is complete.

**AI Role:** AI can help structure teaching content — outlines, formatting, organizing steps. Human must validate accuracy and completeness. Teaching materials that contain errors propagate those errors to every future learner.

**Output:** A published teaching artifact — an SOP in the Mainframe, a video in the Academy, a course module, or a documented mentorship session.

**CCS Progression:** This is where LDT and CCS intersect. The teaching output starts as Content (a written explanation or video), gets refined into a Checklist (step-by-step execution guide), and eventually becomes Software (an automated workflow or tool). Not every skill will reach the Software stage, but every skill should reach Checklist.

**Example:** After mastering article writing, [Your Content Specialist] writes an updated version of the Article Writing SOP that includes the pitfalls he discovered and the shortcuts he found. That SOP becomes the Learn material for the next Content Specialist.

## 3. Skill Levels (SBP Framework)

LDT maps directly to the SBP triangle. Completing LDT cycles is how people advance through levels.

### Specialist (Level 1)

- Has completed LDT for 1-3 specific tasks
- Can execute reliably from checklists
- Needs supervision on new task types
- Typical: new VA, apprentice in first 60 days, [Your Content Specialist] in his first month
- Course format: MICRO courses — task-specific, checklist-heavy

### Business (Level 2)

- Has completed LDT for an entire workflow (e.g., full Content Factory pipeline from transcript to published article)
- Can manage a project end-to-end without daily direction
- Can make judgment calls within the workflow (not just follow steps)
- Typical: experienced team member, 6+ months, Content Specialist who can run a Sprint's content deliverables independently
- Course format: Workflow-level courses — 1-2 hours, includes decision-making frameworks

### Partner (Level 3)

- Has completed LDT for multiple workflows AND has taught others successfully
- Can design new processes and improve existing ones
- Can onboard and train new team members
- Typical: senior team member, leadership track, [Your Ops Partner]-level operations ownership
- Course format: Strategy-level courses — case-study based, includes teaching methodology

**Advancement Rule:** Level advancement requires approval from [Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor]. Self-assessment is not sufficient. The Role Scorecards define the quantitative metrics; LDT level defines the qualitative competency.

## 4. How to Track LDT Progress

Create a simple matrix for each team member. Rows are skills, columns are L/D/T status.

### Format

```
| Skill                  | Learn | Do (reps) | Teach          | Level      |
|------------------------|-------|-----------|----------------|------------|
| Article Writing        | done  | done (5/3)| SOP written    | Business   |
| Dollar-a-Day           | done  | 2/3       | --             | Specialist |
| Client Onboarding      | done  | done (3/3)| --             | Specialist |
| MAA Reporting          | done  | done (4/3)| Mentored [Your Ops Partner] | Business   |
| Content Factory (full) | done  | done (3/3)| Course module  | Partner    |
```

### Tracking Rules

- **Learn:** Mark as "done" when the written summary is submitted and approved
- **Do:** Track rep count as actual/required (e.g., "2/3" means 2 of 3 required reps completed). Only count reps that passed QA without major revisions.
- **Teach:** Record what teaching artifact was created. "SOP written," "Video recorded," "Mentored [name]," etc.
- **Level:** Determined by the breadth of completed LDT cycles, per the SBP definitions above
- **Update frequency:** Weekly during onboarding (first 60 days), monthly for established team members
- **Storage:** Each team member's LDT matrix lives in their Basecamp project or in the Mainframe under `team-ops/`

## 5. How to Structure MICRO Courses

Every skill that gets taught should have a corresponding course. Course complexity scales with the target level.

### Level 1 — Specialist Courses

- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes
- **Format:** Checklist-heavy, screen-share walkthrough
- **Structure:**
  1. **Context** (2 min): What is this skill? Why does it matter? Where does it fit in the Content Factory?
  2. **Steps** (10-20 min): Exact step-by-step execution with screenshots or screen recording. Every step tied to a checklist item.
  3. **Practice** (5 min): One guided exercise with expected output defined
  4. **Assessment** (5 min): Quiz or verbal walkthrough — can you explain the process and identify the quality gates?
- **Example:** "How to Write an Article from a Transcript" — watch the video, read the SOP, follow the checklist, write one article, submit for review.

### Level 2 — Business Courses

- **Duration:** 1-2 hours
- **Format:** Workflow-level, includes decision trees and judgment calls
- **Structure:**
  1. **Context** (10 min): How this workflow fits into the larger system. What happens before and after your part.
  2. **Steps** (30-60 min): End-to-end workflow with branching logic. "If X, do Y. If Z, escalate."
  3. **Practice** (20-30 min): Multi-step exercise that requires judgment, not just execution
  4. **Assessment** (15 min): Scenario-based — "A client sends you this. What do you do and why?"
- **Example:** "Running a Content Factory Sprint End-to-End" — from transcript gathering through publishing, including QA, revision cycles, and client communication.

### Level 3 — Partner Courses

- **Duration:** 2-4 hours (can be split across sessions)
- **Format:** Strategy-level, case-study based, includes teaching methodology
- **Structure:**
  1. **Context** (15 min): The strategic framework. Why this process exists. How it connects to the 9 Triangles.
  2. **Case Studies** (60-90 min): 2-3 real examples — what went right, what went wrong, what was learned
  3. **Practice** (30-60 min): Design a training plan for a new team member learning this skill. Build the LDT progression.
  4. **Assessment** (30 min): Teach a portion of the course to a peer or new hire. Evaluated on clarity, accuracy, and completeness.
- **Example:** "Designing and Managing a Content Factory for a New Client" — from GCT discovery through ongoing MAA reporting, including how to train a Specialist to handle the execution.

## 6. Human Gates

Every phase of LDT has a mandatory human gate. AI can assist throughout, but these checkpoints require human judgment.

| Phase | Gate | Who | What They Check |
|-------|------|-----|-----------------|
| **LEARN** | Comprehension verification | Direct manager ([Your Name], [Your Ops Partner]) | Does the person actually understand, or did they just read/watch passively? Can they explain WHY, not just WHAT? |
| **DO** | Output quality review | Direct manager + QA reviewer | Do the 3 reps meet quality standards? Are they consistent? Would you publish this without changes? |
| **TEACH** | Teaching material validation | [Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor] | Is the teaching artifact accurate? Complete? Good enough for someone else to Learn from? Does it match the canon? |
| **Level advancement** | Promotion approval | [Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor] | Has the person earned the next level through demonstrated LDT completion across sufficient skills? |

**Why humans, not AI:** AI can check mechanical quality (formatting, spelling, structure). It cannot assess true comprehension, voice authenticity, strategic judgment, or teaching effectiveness. These are the exact areas where [Your Mentor/Advisor] has pushed back hardest — the [Your Content Specialist] AI language incident proved that automated checks miss what a trained human eye catches.

## Quick Reference: Designing a Training Plan for Any Skill

Use this checklist when setting up LDT for a new skill:

1. **Define the skill clearly.** One skill per LDT cycle. "Content Factory" is too broad. "Writing an article from a transcript" is right.
2. **Gather Learn materials.** What SOPs, videos, courses, or examples already exist? If nothing exists, the trainer must create the Learn materials first (which is their own Teach phase for that skill).
3. **Set DO criteria.** How many reps? What does "passing QA" look like? Who reviews?
4. **Define the Teach output.** What artifact will the learner create? SOP update, video walkthrough, mentorship session?
5. **Assign the human gates.** Who approves Learn completion? Who reviews DO reps? Who validates the Teach artifact?
6. **Build the tracking row.** Add the skill to the team member's LDT matrix.
7. **Set the timeline.** When should each phase be complete? Use the duration guidelines from the Learn phase section.
8. **Start.** Do not over-plan. The person should be in the Learn phase within 24 hours of the plan being created.
