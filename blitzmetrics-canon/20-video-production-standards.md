# Video Production Standards — [Methodology Partner] Canon

**Source:** Master Video Editing Process Guide (v1.5), Master Content Factory Guide (CoachYu v9.9), DAD Webinar Deck, [Your Mentor/Advisor] training
**Version:** 1.0 | **Extracted:** 2026-04-01

## Core Principle

Video "processing" follows checklists. No fancy effects, b-rolls, or sound effects needed. A "video editor" is NOT required. Video processing serves GCT through the Content Factory stages. The purpose is to capture authentic expertise and distribute it efficiently -- not to create polished TV spots.

---

## WHY Video Formula (3 Components)

The WHY Video is the foundational personal brand video. It establishes emotional connection and answers "Why should I care about this person?"

### Component 1: "When I was..."
Get right to the story. Waste no time. This is where the WHY comes in. Share a defining moment, struggle, or turning point.

### Component 2: "I believe that..."
Go from the emotion of the story to the overarching lesson. State what you stand for. This bridges personal experience to universal principle.

### Component 3: "I am..." (or "I started...")
Give a brief, specific, concise explanation of what you do. Be specific about WHO you serve and HOW you serve them. No more than 2-3 sentences.

### Example Script Structure
> "When I was running my first business, I watched three contractors get destroyed by fake SEO agencies who took their money and delivered nothing. I believe that honest business owners deserve honest marketing -- results they can see, measured by phone calls and revenue, not vanity metrics. I started [Your Agency] to build real digital authority for founder-led businesses using the same system that's worked for Nike, the Golden State Warriors, and hundreds of local contractors."

## One-Minute Video Structure (4 Components)

The standard format for all short-form content across the 3x3 Video Grid.

### Component 1: Hook (0-3 seconds)
- Capture attention immediately
- No video bumper, no saying your name, no recreating a TV spot
- Get right to it -- the hook determines whether anyone watches the rest
- Use a question, a bold statement, or a surprising fact

### Component 2: Ignite Pain/Pleasure (3-15 seconds)
- Users must identify with the problem or opportunity
- Ask a question, show success or failure, make the benefit clear
- This is where the viewer decides "this is relevant to me"

### Component 3: Describe Solution (15-50 seconds)
- What are you offering? What is the product or service?
- Show the process, the results, or the transformation
- Keep it concrete and specific -- real examples beat abstractions

### Component 4: Call to Action (50-60 seconds)
- What do you want them to do? Be specific
- One CTA only -- do not give three options
- Link in bio, DM me, visit this URL, book a call

## 3x3 Video Grid

A 9-video funnel system organized into three stages, aligned with the Topic Wheel (WHY/HOW/WHAT).

### WHY Videos (Videos 1-3) — Top of Funnel
Establish authority and emotional connection.
- Founder stories and origin narratives
- User testimonials from real customers
- Third-party endorsements from recognized names
- Purpose: Get attention, build trust, create meaningful first touch

### HOW Videos (Videos 4-6) — Middle of Funnel
Teach practical solutions WITHOUT mentioning your product.
- Demonstrate what you would do to solve the problem if your product did not exist
- Simple 1-2-3 recipes for getting something done
- Educational content that builds credibility
- Purpose: Demonstrate expertise, build deeper trust

### WHAT Videos (Videos 7-9) — Bottom of Funnel
Product-focused content.
- Services, pricing, promotions
- Customer testimonials about results
- Features and benefits with direct CTA
- Purpose: Convert warm audience to customers

### Sequencing Logic (for Dollar-a-Day)
1. Cold audience sees WHY content first
2. Upon 15+ seconds of WHY engagement, they receive HOW videos alongside additional WHY content
3. After 15+ seconds of HOW engagement, they transition to WHAT videos
4. People should only see videos from their current stage AND the next stage

### Retargeting Ad Set Structure
- **Ad Set 1:** WHY videos to lookalike audiences, EXCLUDING WHAT video viewers
- **Ad Set 2:** HOW videos, EXCLUDING viewers who watched WHAT content
- **Ad Set 3:** WHAT videos with no exclusions
- **Ad Set 4:** Continued WHAT videos until conversion

## 10-Snippet Repurposing from Long-Form

From one interview or long-form video, extract exactly 10 snippets:

| Type | Count | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Edification clips | 2 | Third-party endorsements, authority builders |
| Problem/Solution clips | 5 | Core teaching content |
| Hook clips | 3 | Attention-grabbing moments for scroll-stopping |

The edification clips bookend the problem/solution clips. Hooks go at the end for maximum scroll-stopping power.

### Rule of 10 (Video Application)
1. **10% engagement rate** -- Target benchmark. Content + audience hitting 10%+ is a winner.
2. **10 snippets from 1 interview** -- Every long-form piece yields at least 10 short-form snippets.
3. **1 in 10 ads is a winner** -- Run 10, find the 1 winner, kill the rest.

## Video Quality Checklist

Every processed video must pass these gates before posting:

### Format
- [ ] Square (1:1) or portrait format (4:5 at 1080x1350 or 9:16 at 1080x1920)
- [ ] No intro bumper
- [ ] No copyrighted music or images

### Visual
- [ ] Lower third with name displayed for 5 seconds at beginning
- [ ] Do not cut off head in frame
- [ ] Good lighting -- face clearly visible
- [ ] Clean background (no clutter, no distracting elements)

### Audio
- [ ] Clear audio -- use Studio Sound in Descript if needed
- [ ] No background noise or music competing with speech

### Captions
- [ ] Captions included (required for all social platforms)
- [ ] Captions centered and readable (white text)
- [ ] Maximum 3 lines of caption text at any time
- [ ] No "Uh" / "Um" / "Hm" in captions
- [ ] Captions positioned at bottom in 1:5 or 4:5 ratio
- [ ] Captions occupy no more than 20% of frame

### Content
- [ ] Remove stuttering and dead air
- [ ] Remove "I'm nervous" disclaimers
- [ ] Do not start sentences with "so"
- [ ] No filler words (use Descript's filler word removal)

## Video Processing vs Video Editing

This distinction is critical to the Content Factory model:

**Video Processing** (what we do):
- Follow checklists systematically
- Cut, trim, add captions, apply lower thirds
- No creative decisions required
- A Level 1 VA can handle this
- Serves the GCT through Content Factory stages

**Video Editing** (what we do NOT do):
- Fancy transitions and effects
- B-roll footage and cutaways
- Sound effects and music beds
- Color grading and visual effects
- Requires creative judgment and expensive talent

The system is designed so that a "video editor" is NOT required. Anyone who can follow a checklist can process videos.

## 3 Components of Authority (Video Scoring)

Score every video on a 30-point scale before deciding what to amplify:

1. **Proof** (1-10) -- Certifications, awards, media mentions, case studies with real numbers
2. **People** (1-10) -- Who endorses you? Third-party validation from recognized names
3. **Properties** (1-10) -- Where does the content live? High-authority platforms and domains

Shoot for 10s in each. Boost highest-scoring content via Dollar-a-Day.

---

## Human Requirements

1. **Raw content must come from real humans** -- the client or subject must appear on camera; AI-generated video faces are not acceptable
2. **WHY Video script development requires human reflection** -- the "When I was..." story must be authentic
3. **Quality review before posting** -- a human must watch every processed video before it goes live
4. **Snippet selection from long-form** -- humans identify the best 10 moments; AI can assist but not replace judgment on what resonates
5. **Caption accuracy review** -- auto-generated captions must be proofread by a human
