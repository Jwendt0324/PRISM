## Overview

The Topic Wheel maps WHO you know (your network) with WHAT you know (your expertise). It organizes all content into a structured framework that moves from emotional connection to practical teaching to commercial conversion.

---

## Structure: WHY → HOW → WHAT

### WHY (Outer Ring) — Stories & Connection
- One-minute stories about what you stand for
- Interview OTHER people about their stories
- Cluster stories into different topics
- Leverage the power of other people's networks
- Purpose: Help people understand WHO you are and WHY you do what you do
- NOT selling. NOT teaching. Just connecting on shared values.

### HOW (Middle Ring) — Topics & Teaching
- Topics related to your product/service but NOT directly about it
- Teach via simple recipes and steps
- Interview experts on broader industry issues
- Answer PAA (People Also Ask) questions
- Purpose: Demonstrate expertise and build consideration

### WHAT (Center) — Product/Service & Conversion
- The product or service you're selling
- Testimonials, case studies, pricing
- Features, benefits, comparisons
- Purpose: Convert consideration into action

---

## Building Your Topic Wheel

1. **Start from strength:**
   - Introverts: Start with topics you're passionate about → associate with people
   - Extroverts: Start with people you know → map to expertise areas

2. **Map goals to topics to people:**
   - To sell more, map goals → topics → authoritative people in those topics
   - People connect with WHY, want to understand HOW, then care about WHAT

3. **Identify 6-12 topics** that surround your core offering
   - Each topic should have multiple WHY stories from different people
   - Each topic maps to specific PAA questions
   - Each topic connects to your WHAT

---

## Topic Wheel and Content Organization

- **Website:** Blog categories should mirror Topic Wheel topics
- **Content Library:** Every piece tagged to its Topic Wheel position
- **3x3 Video Grid:** 3 WHY videos, 3 HOW videos, 3 WHAT videos
- **Articles:** Organized by topic, internally linked within topics
- **Social posts:** Distributed across topics for balanced coverage

## 3 Components of Authority

Score every piece of content on a 30-point scale:

1. **WHAT was said** (content quality) — 1-10
2. **WHO said it** (person's authority) — 1-10
3. **WHERE it was posted** (property authority) — 1-10

The most authoritative person saying the most authoritative content on the most authoritative property = maximum leverage.

Example: [Your Mentor/Advisor] on CNN discussing Facebook data (10+10+10 = 30) vs. unknown person on personal blog about same topic (5+2+2 = 9).

Track authority scores in Content Library. Boost highest-scoring content via Dollar-a-Day.
