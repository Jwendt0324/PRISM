## Purpose

GCT (Goals, Content, Targeting) must be completed before any Content Factory work begins. The canon defines WHAT GCT is (see 01-nine-triangles.md), but not HOW to facilitate it. This document fills that gap.

This is the implementation guide for GCT discovery — the step-by-step process for preparing, conducting, and documenting a GCT session with a client. A VA can follow this to prep for a GCT call. A senior team member ([Your Name] or equivalent) must facilitate the actual discovery conversation.

---

## The GCT Framework — Quick Recap

GCT is the first triangle every client encounters. Nothing happens without it.

- **Goals:** What the client wants to achieve. Mission, desired outcomes, customer segments, CPA/ROAS targets, key metrics per ACC stage.
- **Content:** What content exists and what needs to be created. Inventory of assets, Topic Wheel topics, content gaps, creation capacity.
- **Targeting:** Who the audience is and how to reach them. Custom audiences, interest/competitor targets, lookalikes, funnel sequences, geography.

**Canon reference:** "If you don't understand the GCT, the guide will be useless." — Master Content Factory Guide

## Discovery Process

### Step 1: Pre-Call Prep (VA can do this)

Before the discovery call, research the client's existing presence. Document findings in a prep sheet to bring into the call.

**Research checklist:**
- [ ] Website audit — load speed, mobile-friendly, schema markup, blog presence, calls to action
- [ ] Google Business Profile — verified? Reviews? Posts? Categories correct?
- [ ] Facebook — personal profile activity, business page (likes, engagement, post frequency)
- [ ] LinkedIn — profile completeness, posting frequency, connections/followers
- [ ] YouTube — channel exists? Subscriber count, video count, recent uploads
- [ ] Instagram/TikTok — presence, follower count, content style
- [ ] Google Search Console / Analytics — request access before the call if possible
- [ ] Competitor scan — identify 3 competitors, note what they're doing well online
- [ ] People Also Ask (PAA) — pull 10-20 PAA questions for their primary keyword/industry
- [ ] Existing content inventory — any podcasts, speaking engagements, press mentions, testimonials visible online

**Output:** A 1-page prep sheet summarizing what you found, organized by Goals/Content/Targeting. Bring this into the discovery call so you're not starting from zero.

---

### Step 2: Discovery Questions — GOALS

These questions determine what the client wants to achieve. Organize by business type if needed (local service, e-commerce, personal brand, B2B).

**Core questions:**
1. What are your top 3 business goals for the next 90 days?
2. What does success look like? (Get specific metrics — revenue target, lead count, appointment volume, etc.)
3. What have you tried before? What worked? What didn't?
4. Who are your top 3 competitors? What are they doing that you want to do (or avoid)?
5. What's your monthly budget for content and ads?
6. Do you have a CPA (cost per acquisition) target or ROAS (return on ad spend) goal?
7. What's your average customer lifetime value?
8. Is there a specific product/service you want to push, or is it brand-level?

**Listen for:**
- Clarity vs. vagueness — if the client can't articulate goals, they're not ready for Content Factory
- Unrealistic expectations — "$500/month budget, want 10x revenue" needs calibration
- Past failures — what went wrong tells you what to avoid
- Urgency signals — are they in growth mode or survival mode?

---

### Step 3: Discovery Questions — CONTENT

These questions determine what content exists and what needs to be created.

**Core questions:**
1. What content do you already have? (Videos, articles, podcasts, photos, case studies, presentations)
2. Do you have a Topic Wheel defined? If not, what are your 6-12 areas of expertise?
3. What's your preferred content format? (Video, written, audio, visual)
4. Do you have existing testimonials, case studies, or social proof?
5. What's your content creation capacity? (Hours per week the owner can dedicate to producing raw content)
6. Do you have a blog on your website? How frequently is it updated?
7. Do you have any existing email sequences or newsletters?
8. Have you ever been on a podcast, spoken at an event, or been featured in media?
9. Do you have a library of photos from your work, events, or team? (Geotagged?)
10. Who on your team (if anyone) currently handles content?

**Listen for:**
- Content goldmines — hours of unprocessed video, years of photos, past speaking engagements
- Content deserts — no content at all means you need to start at Stage 1 (Produce) from scratch
- Owner willingness — the business owner MUST produce the 1% (raw content). If they resist, flag it.
- Topic Wheel fit — can you identify 6-12 clear expertise areas from the conversation?

---

### Step 4: Discovery Questions — TARGETING

These questions determine who the audience is and how to reach them.

**Core questions:**
1. Who is your ideal customer? (Demographics: age, income, job title, industry)
2. Describe your ideal customer's mindset — what problem keeps them up at night?
3. Where do they spend time online? (Facebook groups, LinkedIn, YouTube, Reddit, industry forums)
4. What's your current email list size? Is it segmented?
5. Do you have existing custom audiences? (Facebook pixel data, Google remarketing, customer email lists)
6. What geographic area do you serve? (Local, regional, national, international)
7. Do you have a CRM? What's in it? (Customer data, lead data, purchase history)
8. What's your current monthly website traffic?
9. Have you run paid ads before? On which platforms? What was the result?
10. Who are the influencers, thought leaders, or brands your ideal customer follows?

**Listen for:**
- Existing audience data — customer email lists are gold for custom audiences and lookalikes
- Platform alignment — where the audience is determines where you post and promote
- Geographic constraints — local businesses need different targeting than national brands
- Pixel/remarketing history — existing pixel data accelerates everything

## Scoping Based on GCT

After completing discovery, use GCT findings to determine project scope and starting point.

### Decision Matrix

| Goals | Content | Targeting | Recommendation | Starting Point |
|-------|---------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Clear | Exists | Defined | Ready for Content Factory | Stage 2 (Process) — process existing content |
| Clear | None | Defined | Need Plumbing + Produce first | Stage 0 (Plumbing) + Stage 1 (Produce) |
| Clear | Exists | Undefined | Need audience research + targeting setup | Stage 0 (Plumbing) — build audiences |
| Clear | None | Undefined | Full build required | Stage 0 (Plumbing) — full setup |
| Unclear | Any | Any | **Not ready.** Do strategy session first. | No Content Factory yet |

### Engagement Type Mapping

| Client Situation | Recommended Engagement |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Clear GCT + budget + committed to content creation | Ongoing retainer (Content Factory) |
| Clear goals + needs quick authority/visibility win | KP Sprint (Knowledge Panel Sprint) |
| Has content + needs amplification only | Dollar-a-Day only |
| Unclear goals + needs strategic clarity | Strategy session (paid, 1-2 hours) — then re-evaluate |
| Low budget + willing to learn | AI Apprentice Program (self-service with coaching) |

### Red Flags (Do Not Proceed)

- Client cannot articulate any business goals
- Client refuses to produce any raw content ("just do it all for me")
- Budget is below minimum viable threshold for their goals
- Client wants to skip GCT ("just start running ads")
- Client has unrealistic timeline expectations with no flexibility

## GCT Output Format

The completed GCT document is the deliverable from discovery. Every client must have one before work begins.

```
## Client: [Name]
## Date: [Date]
## Facilitator: [Who ran the discovery call]

### GOALS
- Primary goal: [specific, measurable — e.g., "Generate 20 qualified leads/month for hearing aid consultations"]
- Secondary goal: [e.g., "Establish Dr. Smith as the go-to audiologist in Phoenix metro"]
- 90-day target: [what success looks like in 90 days]
- Monthly budget: [content + ads combined]
- CPA/ROAS target: [if applicable]
- Key metrics: [what we'll track — appointments booked, website traffic, social engagement, etc.]

### CONTENT
- Existing assets: [inventory — e.g., "47 YouTube videos, 12 blog posts, 200+ patient photos"]
- Topic Wheel topics: [6-12 topics — e.g., "hearing aids, tinnitus, pediatric audiology, cochlear implants, hearing protection, musician ear molds"]
- Content gaps: [what's missing — e.g., "No WHY video, no testimonial videos, no PAA content"]
- Creation capacity: [hours/week owner can dedicate — e.g., "2 hours/week for filming"]
- Preferred format: [video, written, audio, mixed]
- Content team: [who handles content now, if anyone]

### TARGETING
- Ideal customer: [profile — e.g., "Adults 55+, household income $75K+, experiencing hearing loss, within 30 miles of Phoenix"]
- Psychographics: [mindset — e.g., "Frustrated by inability to hear in social situations, worried about cognitive decline"]
- Platforms: [where they are — e.g., "Facebook (primary), YouTube (research phase), Google Search"]
- Existing audiences: [e.g., "3,200 customer email list, Facebook pixel with 12 months of data"]
- Geography: [e.g., "Phoenix metro area, 30-mile radius"]
- Influencers/brands they follow: [e.g., "AARP, local health publications, Dr. Cliff AuD on YouTube"]

### NEXT STEPS
- Recommended engagement: [retainer / KP Sprint / Dollar-a-Day / strategy session]
- Starting stage: [Stage 0 / Stage 1 / Stage 2]
- Immediate actions: [e.g., "1. Set up access via [methodology-partner.com]/acl 2. Film WHY video 3. Export customer email list"]
- Timeline: [when work begins, first deliverable target]
```

## Human Gates

GCT discovery MUST be facilitated by a human. This is non-negotiable.

| Step | Who | Why |
|------|-----|-----|
| Pre-call research | VA or AI | Fact-gathering is delegatable |
| Discovery call facilitation | [Your Name] or senior team member | Requires judgment, rapport, reading between the lines |
| Follow-up questions | [Your Name] or senior team member | Probing unclear answers requires human intuition |
| GCT document drafting | VA or AI | Structuring answers into the template is delegatable |
| GCT document review | [Your Name] | Final sign-off ensures nothing was missed or misinterpreted |
| Scoping recommendation | [Your Name] | Engagement type and pricing require business judgment |
| Pricing discussion | [Your Name] | No discounting without [Your Name]'s approval (see 08-human-requirements.md) |

**What AI CAN do:**
- Pre-call research (website audit, social scan, competitor analysis, PAA pulls)
- Draft the GCT document from call notes or recording transcript
- Suggest Topic Wheel topics based on content inventory
- Pull PAA questions for the client's industry
- Draft the prep sheet before the call

**What AI CANNOT do:**
- Facilitate the discovery conversation
- Make scoping or pricing decisions
- Assess client readiness or commitment level
- Read between the lines on vague answers
- Build rapport or trust with the client

## Anti-Vandalism

These rules prevent damage to the client relationship and downstream work quality.

- **Never skip GCT.** No GCT = no Content Factory work. Period. If someone pushes to "just get started," escalate to [Your Name].
- **Never fabricate GCT answers.** If a question wasn't answered in discovery, mark it as "TBD — follow up required." Do not guess.
- **Never start Content Factory stages without a completed GCT document.** The GCT doc gates Stage 0 (Plumbing) and everything after it.
- **Never let a client skip the Goals section.** "Just run some ads" is not a goal. Push back respectfully and escalate if needed.
- **Never commit to pricing or scope during the discovery call itself.** Discovery is for gathering information. Scoping and pricing happen after GCT review.
- **Never share the pre-call prep sheet with the client.** It's an internal tool for the facilitator.
