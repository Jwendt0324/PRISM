## Overview

This canon file consolidates three audit methodologies into a single reference:

1. **Quick Audit** -- 12-point website and social media checklist ($297 service, 30-minute deliverable)
2. **E-E-A-T Scorecard** -- Deep technical audit with letter grading (used for KP Sprint assessments)

The Quick Audit qualifies leads and demonstrates value. It feeds into the KP Sprint ($6K engagement) for businesses that need deeper work.

**Pipeline:** Quick Audit ($297) --> KP Sprint ($6K) --> Ongoing Content Factory + MAA

## Quick Audit: 12-Point Website Checklist

These are the 12 most common website mistakes found across hundreds of audits. Check every one.

### Content & Messaging
1. **No "subscribe" or "give me your email" CTAs** -- Use "newsletter," "tips," or "weekly insights" instead. "Subscribe" signals spam.
2. **No red buttons** -- Red is a warning/danger signal. Use blue, green, or brand-appropriate colors for CTAs.
3. **Use real photos and videos, never stock photos** -- Stock photos destroy trust. Every photo should be of real people, real work, real locations.
4. **No arms crossed in photos** -- Arms crossed signals standoffishness and defensiveness. Open posture only.
5. **Make clear what you want people to do** -- Every page needs ONE primary CTA. If the visitor does not know what to do, they leave.
6. **Do not put CTA buttons above the fold before establishing trust** -- Lead with value and credibility. Earn the click before asking for it.
7. **Position as unique access, not just "more content"** -- YouTube is free. Your offering must provide something they cannot get elsewhere.

### Technical & Structural
8. **Social media buttons must link to actual profiles** -- Dead social links or links to platform homepages waste link juice and look abandoned.
9. **Cross traffic between channels** -- Social profiles link to website. Website links to social. Email links to both. Every channel feeds every other channel.
10. **Podcast appearances must link back to your website** -- Every external appearance should generate an inbound link.
11. **Build and monetize email list** -- Email is the only owned channel. Social platforms can change algorithms overnight.
12. **Native video posting on each platform** -- Upload videos natively to each platform. Never post a YouTube link on Facebook -- "like driving a Honda into a Toyota dealership."

## Social Media Audit Checklist

Run alongside the website audit.

### Per-Platform Check
- [ ] Profile photo: real, professional, consistent across platforms
- [ ] Bio: clear, includes what you do and who you serve
- [ ] Website link: present and functional
- [ ] Recent activity: posted within last 7 days
- [ ] Engagement: comments and replies, not just broadcasting
- [ ] Video: native uploads, not cross-posted links

### LinkedIn-Specific
- [ ] Headline uses XYZ format: "I help [X] achieve [Y] through [Z]"
- [ ] Featured section populated with best content
- [ ] Experience section complete with descriptions
- [ ] Recommendations section has recent endorsements

### Cross-Platform Consistency
- [ ] Same name/handle format across all platforms
- [ ] Same profile photo everywhere
- [ ] Same core bio message (adjusted for character limits)
- [ ] Bidirectional linking (website <--> social profiles)

## E-E-A-T Scorecard

The deep audit format used for KP Sprint assessments and comprehensive SEO analysis. Uses letter grading to make results immediately understandable to non-technical clients.

### Grading Scale

| Grade | Meaning | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| A | Excellent | Industry-leading, exceeds expectations |
| B | Good | Solid foundation, minor improvements needed |
| C | Fair | Functional but significant gaps exist |
| D | Poor | Major issues that actively harm performance |
| F | Failing | Broken, missing, or counterproductive |

Use + and - modifiers (D+, C-, B+) for precision.

### Scorecard Categories

#### 1. Experience (E)
Does the content demonstrate first-hand experience with the subject?
- Real job photos and videos from actual work
- Customer interactions documented
- Behind-the-scenes content showing process
- Location-specific content proving presence in service area
- Geotagged media

**Grade criteria:**
- A: Extensive library of real work documentation across multiple formats
- B: Consistent real content but gaps in coverage
- C: Some real content mixed with generic/stock content
- D: Mostly generic content with minimal proof of real experience
- F: No evidence of real experience; all content is stock or AI-generated

#### 2. Expertise (E)
Does the content demonstrate deep knowledge of the subject?
- Technical accuracy of content
- Depth of topic coverage (surface-level vs comprehensive)
- Content created by subject matter experts vs freelance generalists
- Certifications, licenses, and qualifications referenced
- HOW content in the Topic Wheel framework

**Grade criteria:**
- A: Content clearly authored by credentialed experts with demonstrable expertise
- B: Expert-level content with occasional shallow areas
- C: Mix of expert and generic content
- D: Mostly generic content that any competitor could have written
- F: Factually inaccurate or clearly AI-generated without expert review

#### 3. Authoritativeness (A)
Do others recognize this entity as an authority?
- Backlink profile quality (high-DR sites linking in)
- Third-party mentions and citations
- Knowledge Panel presence (or progress toward one)
- Industry association memberships
- Media appearances and publications
- Wikidata entry quality and completeness

**Grade criteria:**
- A: Knowledge Panel active, strong backlink profile (DR 40+), consistent third-party validation
- B: Emerging authority signals, some high-quality backlinks, partial entity recognition
- C: Limited third-party validation, few quality backlinks, no KP
- D: Minimal authority signals, low-quality backlinks, entity not recognized
- F: No authority signals; possible toxic backlink profile

#### 4. Trustworthiness (T)
Can users and Google trust this entity?
- NAP consistency across all platforms
- HTTPS and security configuration
- Review quality and response patterns
- Contact information visibility and accuracy
- Schema markup accuracy
- No deceptive practices (fake reviews, keyword stuffing, cloaking)

**Grade criteria:**
- A: Perfect NAP consistency, 4.5+ stars with 100+ reviews, comprehensive schema, clean backlink profile
- B: Minor NAP inconsistencies, good reviews, basic schema in place
- C: Some trust signals but gaps (missing schema, inconsistent NAP, few reviews)
- D: Major trust issues (wrong NAP, low reviews, no HTTPS, no schema)
- F: Active trust violations (fake reviews, spam links, deceptive practices)

### Scorecard Output Format

| Category | Grade | Key Finding | Priority Fix |
|----------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Experience | C+ | Some real job photos but no video content | Start filming job sites with phone |
| Expertise | D | Blog content from freelancers with no industry knowledge | Implement Content Factory with owner |
| Authoritativeness | D+ | No Knowledge Panel, no high-DR backlinks | Entity foundation + Wikidata entry |
| Trustworthiness | C | NAP inconsistent across 4 directories, schema missing | NAP audit + schema implementation |
| **Overall** | **D+** | **Entity foundation missing, content disconnected** | **Start build order from Step 1** |

## Gemini Audit Model

A structured 6-section audit format designed to be repeatable and GCT-aligned. Can be run with AI assistance but requires human verification.

### Step 0: Define GCT

Before any audit work:
- **Goal:** What are they trying to achieve? (Build authority? Drive local leads? Get a Knowledge Panel?)
- **Content:** What kind of content do they produce currently?
- **Target:** Who is the audience? (Peers? Clients? Homeowners? Investors?)

### Section 1: Profile & Entity Check
- Does Google recognize this business as an entity?
- Knowledge Panel present? (Full, partial, or absent)
- KGMID found? Confidence score?
- Wikidata entry exists?
- NAP consistency across top 20 citations
- Schema markup present and valid

### Section 2: Platform Inventory
- List all active digital properties with URLs
- Note follower counts, activity frequency, last post date
- Flag dormant or abandoned profiles
- Check for unauthorized duplicate listings

### Section 3: Social Deep Dive
- Engagement rates per platform
- Content quality assessment (real vs stock vs AI-generated)
- Cross-posting habits (native vs shared links)
- Branding consistency
- Community engagement (responding to comments, DMs)

### Section 4: Website & Content Analysis
- Domain Rating (DR) benchmarked against top 3 competitors
- PageSpeed score (mobile and desktop)
- Organic keyword rankings -- relevance check
- Homepage vs deep page traffic ratio
- Content ecosystem health (blog connected to YouTube, social, GBP)
- Internal linking structure assessment

### Section 5: Gaps & Risks
- Missing entity signals
- Toxic backlinks or negative SEO indicators
- Content gaps vs competitor coverage
- Technical issues blocking performance
- Security vulnerabilities

### Section 6: KP Readiness Assessment
- Current Knowledge Panel status
- Entity strength score (1-5)
- Content volume and authority score (1-5)
- Google readiness score (1-5)
- Brand risk score (1-5)
- Estimated timeline to KP (if not yet acquired)

### Scoring Table

| Dimension | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Online Authority | | Backlinks, citations, third-party mentions |
| Content Quality | | Real vs generic, depth, consistency |
| Google Readiness | | Schema, entity signals, technical health |
| Brand Risk | | Negative content, inconsistencies, vulnerabilities |

## Google Firefly System

Google's internal system analyzes the ratio of generated URLs to substantive content. When a site has hundreds of location pages with swapped city names (templated content), Firefly flags it as scaled content abuse.

### Detection Signals
- High ratio of thin pages to substantive pages
- Templated content with only location names changed
- No unique photos, videos, or reviews per location
- Identical page structure across all service area pages
- AI content detection signals

### What Firefly Means for Audits
When auditing a site, check for templated location pages. If found, they are a liability, not an asset. The fix is real content per location: actual job photos, customer reviews, neighborhood-specific details.

## Audit Pipeline: Quick Audit to KP Sprint

### Quick Audit ($297)
- 30-minute deliverable
- 12-point website check + social media audit
- Contact page optimization review
- 5-step action plan
- Purpose: Demonstrate value, qualify the lead, identify severity of issues

### Bridge Conversation
- Review Quick Audit findings with prospect
- Identify which issues require professional intervention
- Scope the KP Sprint based on specific gaps found
- Set 90-day expectations

### KP Sprint ($6,000)
- 90-day engagement
- Full E-E-A-T Scorecard + Gemini Audit as baseline
- Entity foundation build (Wikidata, schema, NAP)
- Content Factory setup
- Weekly MAA reporting
- Knowledge Panel acquisition target
- Geo grid tracking (for local businesses)

### Ongoing Engagement
- Monthly Content Factory execution
- Weekly MAA reports
- Quarterly E-E-A-T re-scoring to show progress
- Dollar-a-Day amplification of winning content

## Human Requirements

1. **Quick Audit delivery requires human presentation** -- walking through findings in person or on video builds trust that a PDF cannot
2. **E-E-A-T grading requires human judgment** -- the difference between a C and a D is contextual; AI can flag signals but humans must weigh them
3. **Competitor analysis requires market knowledge** -- understanding who the real competitors are (not just who ranks) requires business context
4. **Firefly / content abuse assessment requires editorial judgment** -- determining whether content is "real enough" vs templated is a qualitative call
5. **Pipeline conversion requires human sales conversation** -- the Quick Audit demonstrates value but the KP Sprint sale requires a human who understands the prospect's pain
6. **Action plan prioritization requires business context** -- the technically correct priority may not match the client's business priorities or budget constraints
