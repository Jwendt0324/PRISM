# Google Maps 3-Pack Methodology — [Methodology Partner] Canon

**Source:** "How to Get Into Google's 3-Pack" (Luke Crowson), Marketing Mechanic Ep 16 & 19, Local SEO teachings, Are You Googleable ([Vertical Lighthouse] guide)
**Version:** 1.0 | **Extracted:** 2026-04-01

---

## Core Principle

Google Maps rankings are entity-driven, not keyword-driven. A business must exist as a recognized entity connected to both its industry vertical AND geographic location before map pack tactics will work. Most contractor SEO fails because it skips the entity foundation entirely.

---

## Non-Negotiable Build Order

This sequence cannot be rearranged. Each step depends on the previous one.

1. **Entity Foundation** -- Establish who you are in Google's Knowledge Graph (consistent NAP, Wikidata, schema)
2. **Digital Plumbing** -- GA4, GSC, schema markup, tracking pixels, call tracking ([[blitzmetrics-canon/22-digital-plumbing-checklist|Digital Plumbing Checklist]])
3. **Geo-Vertical Grid** -- Connect to industry vertical AND geographic location (see below)
4. **Content Factory** -- Real content from real work, repurposed across platforms
5. **MAA Optimization** -- Metrics, Analysis, Action on what drives calls ([[blitzmetrics-canon/05-maa-framework|MAA Framework]])
6. **Personal Brand** -- Owner's Knowledge Panel linked to company entity
7. **Dollar-a-Day Amplification** -- Boost winners, not everything ([[blitzmetrics-canon/04-dollar-a-day|Dollar-a-Day Strategy]])

---

## Qualification Checklist

Before spending money on a geo grid / map pack campaign, the business must pass ALL of these:

- [ ] Category where Google Maps visibility directly drives revenue (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, landscaping, dental, legal)
- [ ] Verified physical location (not a virtual office or P.O. box)
- [ ] 100+ Google reviews minimum
- [ ] Average ticket value above $150
- [ ] Claimed, optimized Google Business Profile
- [ ] Live call answering capability (missed calls = wasted rankings)
- [ ] Willing to create real content (photos/videos from actual job sites)

If a business cannot pass this checklist, they are not ready for a map pack campaign. Start with Digital Plumbing and content production first.

## 10-Keyword Methodology

### Selection Process

1. Pull GBP Insights data for current search queries driving impressions
2. Cross-reference with Google Keyword Planner for volume and competition
3. Select exactly 10 keywords -- not 5, not 20
4. Weight keywords by ticket value, not just search volume:
   - A $15,000 AC replacement keyword at 40 searches/month BEATS a $30 air filter keyword at 200/month
5. Focus on high-intent terms:
   - "Emergency plumber near me" > "how to fix a leaky faucet"
   - "AC repair [city]" > "HVAC maintenance tips"
6. Include a mix of:
   - 3-4 high-volume service keywords (e.g., "plumber [city]", "AC repair [city]")
   - 3-4 high-intent emergency/urgent keywords (e.g., "emergency plumber", "24 hour HVAC")
   - 2-3 high-ticket specialty keywords (e.g., "AC replacement [city]", "sewer line repair")

## Geo Grid Tracking Methodology

### What a Geo Grid Is

A visual map showing your ranking position at every point within your service area. Think of it as a heat map overlaid on Google Maps.

### Color Coding

| Color | Position | Meaning |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Green | 1-3 | In the 3-pack (visible without clicking "More places") |
| Yellow | 4-7 | Close but not visible -- one optimization cycle away |
| Orange | 8-10 | Page 1 of map results but not competitive |
| Red | 11+ | Not visible -- needs foundational work first |

### Operational Process

1. **Baseline scan** before any campaign starts -- this is the "before" snapshot
2. Run scans for all 10 keywords across the service area grid
3. Identify weak zones -- specific geographic areas showing orange/red
4. Target content and citations to weak zones specifically
5. **Post-campaign scan** at 30, 60, 90 days -- compare before/after
6. Include geo grid screenshots in MAA reports as visual proof of progress

### What Geo Grids Show That Rankings Don't

- A business can rank #1 at its physical location but #15 two miles away
- Competitors may dominate certain zip codes while you dominate others
- Service area coverage has geographic gaps that only a grid reveals
- Progress is visible as color changes over time -- clients understand this immediately

## Local Three-Pack Ranking Factors

What Google actually weighs for map pack position:

### Primary Factors

1. **Business Name** -- Must contain relevant keywords AND match the actual legal business name (keyword stuffing triggers suspension)
2. **Primary Category** -- Single most important GBP setting; must match the core service
3. **Web Page** -- The landing page linked from GBP must be locally relevant with proper schema
4. **Reviews** -- Volume, recency, star rating, response rate, keyword mentions in review text

### Entity Signals

- Consistent NAP across website, GBP, social profiles, citations, directories
- Wikidata entry for the business and/or owner
- Schema markup (LocalBusiness, Person, AggregateRating)
- Cross-entity linking between personal brand and company brand

### Geo-Vertical Grid Signals

Two axes create a grid of authority:

**Vertical axis (industry):**
- Equipment manufacturer partnerships
- Trade association memberships
- Training program certifications
- Industry conference participation
- Supplier partnership pages

**Geographic axis (location):**
- Photos of real work in specific neighborhoods (geotagged)
- Engagement with local community pages
- Local news citations
- City-specific content mentioning neighborhoods and landmarks
- Local business relationship links (Chamber of Commerce, BBB)

When both axes are strong, rankings become durable, harder for competitors to replicate, and effective across Search, Maps, and AI results simultaneously.

## Three Reasons Contractor SEO Fails

### 1. No Entity Foundation
GBP, website, social profiles, and citations don't tell a consistent story. Google cannot confidently identify the business as a real entity. Fix: NAP audit, schema markup, Wikidata entry.

### 2. No Geo-Specific Content
Generic pages that could belong to any contractor in any city. Template service area pages with just the city name swapped out. Google's Firefly system flags this as scaled content abuse. Fix: Real photos from real jobs in real cities, customer reviews mentioning specific locations.

### 3. No Measurement of What Drives Calls
Tracking rankings instead of phone calls and booked jobs. A business can rank #1 for terms that generate zero revenue. Fix: Call tracking integrated with ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro, geo grid scans tied to call volume, MAA reporting on calls not impressions.

## GBP Suspension Avoidance

Common triggers for Google Business Profile suspension:

- **Keyword stuffing in business name** -- Adding service keywords that are not part of the legal business name
- **Location spam** -- Creating fake locations or using virtual offices
- **Suggested edits accepted without verification** -- Google allows public suggestions; if not monitored, competitors or bad actors can change your listing
- **Google sweeps** -- Periodic automated enforcement waves that catch violations retroactively

### Prevention
- Business name must match exactly what appears on signage, legal documents, and invoices
- Monitor GBP for suggested edits weekly
- Document everything (photos of signage, business license) in case of suspension appeal
- Never use a virtual office or shared workspace address for a service area business

## MAA Applied to Map Pack Campaigns

Weekly MAA reporting for geo grid campaigns should include:

### Metrics
- Geo grid scan results (visual before/after)
- Call volume by source (organic, Maps, LSA, paid)
- Review count and velocity
- GBP views and actions (calls, directions, website clicks)

### Analysis
- Which keywords improved/declined and why
- Which geographic zones improved/declined
- Correlation between content posted and ranking changes
- Competitor movements in the 3-pack

### Action
- 3-5 specific recommendations per week
- Content assignments targeting weak geographic zones
- Review generation priorities
- Citation or link building targets

## Human Requirements

1. **GBP verification requires a real person at a real address** -- cannot be automated or faked
2. **Job site photos must come from actual technicians** -- AI-generated job photos are detectable and destroy trust
3. **Review responses should be written by a human** -- templated responses look templated; personal responses build relationships
4. **Geo grid analysis requires human interpretation** -- the data shows what changed, but understanding WHY requires local market knowledge
5. **Keyword selection requires business owner input** -- only the owner knows which services are most profitable and which neighborhoods they want to serve
6. **Suspension appeals require human judgment** -- each case is unique; templates get rejected
