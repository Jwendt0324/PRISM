# Entity Linking Decision Tree

**Canon File 17** — Added 2026-04-01 from overnight article scan of [methodology-partner].com

## What It Is

Entity linking is the practice of connecting every mention of a person, business, concept, or organization in content to the most authoritative source about that entity. NOT the same as regular hyperlinking.

- **Regular hyperlink:** "here is more information"
- **Entity link:** "this is the authoritative source for this entity according to Google's Knowledge Graph"

Google uses links to understand relationships between entities. Every link teaches Google what entities are connected.

## The Decision Tree

For every entity mentioned in an article, follow this tree:

### 1. Is it a PERSON?
→ Link to their **personal brand website** (the domain matching their name)
- [Your Mentor/Advisor] → [mentor-site].com
- Ibrahim Awad → ibrahimawad.com
- **NEVER** link to LinkedIn, social media, or a [Methodology Partner] article about them
- The personal brand site IS the canonical entity reference

### 2. Is it a BUSINESS/ORGANIZATION in our network?
→ Link to their **primary website**
- ChurchCandy → their website, NOT the [Methodology Partner] case study about them
- The case study is ABOUT them. Their website IS them.
- Google needs the link pointing TO the entity, not to a page DISCUSSING the entity

### 3. Is it a [METHODOLOGY PARTNER] CONCEPT?
→ Link to its **definitive article** on [methodology-partner].com
- Content Factory → [methodology-partner].com/content-factory/
- Dollar a Day → [methodology-partner].com/the-dollar-a-day-method/
- Digital Plumbing → [methodology-partner].com/digital-plumbing/
- Nine Triangles → [methodology-partner].com/the-9-triangles/
- This is the whole point of the definitive article architecture

### 4. Is it an EXTERNAL concept or well-known entity?
→ Link to the **most authoritative source**
- Google products → official Google page
- Industry concepts → most authoritative industry source
- Public figures → their personal website or official page
- **Do NOT link to Wikipedia** unless no better source exists (minimal value, signals no deeper relationship)

## Linking Rules

1. **Link on first meaningful mention** in the article
2. **Don't re-link** the same entity in the same section (unless very long article, different section)
3. **Use descriptive anchor text** — never bare URLs
4. **Every link has a specific, intentional destination** based on this decision tree

## Common Mistakes

1. Linking a person's name to LinkedIn instead of personal brand site
2. Linking a [Methodology Partner] concept to a case study instead of the definitive article
3. Linking to a competing article when a definitive article exists
4. Using bare URLs instead of descriptive anchor text
5. Over-linking the same entity multiple times in one paragraph
