## What It Is

The SEO Tree is the content architecture framework that connects all [Methodology Partner] content. Think of a website's content like a tree:

| Level | Analogy | Examples |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Root/Trunk** | Homepage, brand entity | [methodology-partner].com, the core identity Google recognizes |
| **Major Branches** | Definitive articles | One per core concept (Content Factory, Dollar-a-Day, Digital Plumbing, Nine Triangles, etc.) |
| **Smaller Branches** | Supporting articles | Deeper subtopics, how-to guides |
| **Leaves** | Case studies, meta-articles | Specific client examples, process documentation |

## How Authority Flows

- **Up the tree:** Leaves strengthen branches. Branches strengthen the trunk.
- **Down the tree:** Higher authority pages provide context for specific content.
- **Across the tree:** Related topics on the same level reinforce each other.

## The 5 Rules

1. **Every piece must connect back through the tree.** No orphan pages.
2. **New content should usually enhance existing content**, not stand alone.
3. **Only create new standalone articles** when you already dominate a topic and want to branch into a subtopic.
4. **Before publishing, answer two questions:** "Which definitive article does this belong to?" and "Does it link back?"
5. **If the answers are "I don't know"**, find the definitive article or create one per the Definitive Article Guide.

## Content Vandalism Connection

Content vandalism happens when content is published without awareness of the knowledge architecture. Specifically:

- A VA repurposes a video into an article without checking if a definitive article exists on that topic
- The new article competes with the definitive article for the same search queries
- Internal linking authority gets diluted
- Google and AI get confused about which page is canonical

**The SEO Tree is the antidote.** Every person and every AI agent who creates content must understand where their content fits in the tree.

## The 4 Content Types

Each [Methodology Partner] concept has 4 distinct content types. Mixing them creates confusion.

| Type | Purpose | Direction |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Definitive Article** | Comprehensive concept explanation. Ranks in search. Free. Hub page. | Other content links UP to this |
| **Course/Guide Page** | Sells training. Pricing, enrollment, testimonials. | Links BACK to definitive article |
| **Case Study** | Documents one specific application (person, business, project). | Links UP to definitive article |
| **Meta-Article** | Documents how content was created using Content Factory. | Links to both definitive article AND original article |
