# The Content Factory Process — BlitzMetrics Canon

**Source:** Master Content Factory Guide v1.7 (2024), Workbook v1.9 (2025), CoachYu Guide v9.9
**Version:** 1.0 | **Extracted:** 2026-03-19

---

## Overview

The Content Factory is the ultimate method to get multiple assets out at once, repurposed across multiple channels. It's not software or an agency — it's a process that can be customized to multiply assets and get the most out of existing tools.

**Core principle:** "Every entrepreneur needs a Content Factory to handle 99% of the work to edit, distribute, boost, and optimize their content. Then, we only need to spend 1% of our time producing it."

**AI principle:** "AI is a multiplier of what you already have. If you start with your actual content, ChatGPT like any tool is an amplifier. Using AI to auto-generate everything is where Google will eventually catch you."

The Content Factory has evolved across versions:

| Version | Stages |

## Stage 0: Plumbing (Setup)

**Purpose:** Set up the foundational digital infrastructure. This is the "setup stage" — done once, maintained ongoing.

**Key components:**
- Google Business Profile (GMB) — properly configured, verified, linked
- Google Analytics — tracking, conversion tracking, behavioral flow
- Google Search Console — verified, monitoring queries
- Remarketing pixels — Facebook, Google, cross-channel
- Website — fast loading, proper structure, schema.org markup
- Social profiles — consistent branding across all platforms
- Access Checklist (blitzmetrics.com/acl) — all platforms granted and documented
- CRM integration — data flows between tools
- Call tracking — connected to analytics

**Quality gate:** All items on the Digital Plumbing Checklist completed. All access granted via blitzmetrics.com/acl. Analytics firing correctly. Remarketing audiences building.

**Human required:** Client must provide business information. Team sets up access.

## Stage 1: Produce

**Purpose:** Generate raw content that proves you do what you say you do, in the places you say you do it in.

**Content types:**
- Zoom/Speaking recordings (webinars, conferences, interviews)
- Podcast recordings (networking with industry peers)
- Social media posts (photos, videos, stories)
- iCloud/Google Photos (geotagged photos from events, work, team)
- One-minute videos (WHY, PAA answers, client stories)
- Three-minute interviews with speakers/authorities

**Quality standards:**
- Content must be AUTHENTIC — real photos, real videos, real locations
- Geotagged media sends trust signals to Google (E-E-A-T)
- Start with WHY video (mission/purpose, 1 minute)
- Answer 50+ PAA (People Also Ask) questions in 1-minute videos
- ONE QUESTION you always ask people (builds your brand)

**The 4 components of a one-minute video:**
1. Hook (0-3 seconds) — capture attention immediately
2. Ignite pain/pleasure (3-15 seconds) — present problem or opportunity
3. Describe solution (15-50 seconds) — show what you offer
4. Call to Action (50-60 seconds) — tell them what to do

**Human required:** Business owner MUST produce raw content. No one else can tell their stories. This is the 1% that the owner must do.

## Stage 2: Process

**Purpose:** Transform raw content into multiple publishable assets using AI tools and VAs.

**Tools:**
- Descript — transcription, video editing, snippet extraction, studio sound
- ChatGPT — content processing, article writing, research (NOT replacement for VA)
- Content Library — Google Sheet tracking all processed assets
- Design Style Guide — standards for visual processing

**Processing pipeline:**
1. Upload raw video to Google Drive
2. Transcribe via Descript
3. Review transcript, correct errors, remove filler
4. Extract key topics and timestamps
5. Write articles from transcript (with E-E-A-T compliance)
6. Create one-minute video snippets
7. Create social media posts
8. Create infographics and visual assets
9. Track ALL processed assets in Content Library

**Quality standards:**
- ChatGPT is an assistant, NOT a replacement — "Remember, ChatGPT is an assistant, not a replacement for your VA"
- Content must be sprinkled with real stories and photos for E-E-A-T
- Active listening skills applied when processing transcripts
- Every processed piece tracked in Content Library

**Human required:** QA review of AI-processed content. Voice/tone approval. E-E-A-T compliance check.

---

## Stage 3: Post

**Purpose:** Distribute processed content across all channels and platforms.

**Distribution channels:**
- Company website (WordPress — organized by Topic Wheel)
- YouTube (long-form and shorts)
- Facebook (profile + public figure page — both, for different reach)
- LinkedIn (broetry style, 10 engagements in first hour = algorithm boost)
- Twitter/X
- TikTok
- Google Business Profile (cross-posting)
- Email sequences

**Quality standards:**
- All content cross-posted to Content Library with URLs
- Consistent branding across platforms
- Blog posts follow article posting guidelines (see 03-article-guidelines.md)
- Featured images for every post
- Schema.org markup on website content
- Internal linking between related content

**Human required:** WordPress publishing (login, featured image, RankMath/SEO). Client approval before publishing to their channels.

## Stage 4: Promote

**Purpose:** Amplify best content using Dollar-a-Day and Thank You Machine.

**Dollar-a-Day:**
- Find organic winners first (1,000+ views or 100+ engagements)
- Phase 1: Test at $1-5/day for 7 days
- Phase 2: Scale winners to $5-20/day for 30 days
- Phase 3: Evergreen "Greatest Hits" rotation at $20-50/day
- See 04-dollar-a-day.md for full detail

**Thank You Machine:**
- Track positive mentions (mention.com or native monitoring)
- Ask permission to quote ("Thank you! Can I quote you on that?" 😉)
- Track in Positive Mention Bank spreadsheet
- Send personalized gifts (BlitzDollars, custom stamps, Amazon gifts, postcards)
- Create content that elevates others (listicles, visual media)
- Amplify client love content for Dollar-a-Day

**Social Commenting & Roundup/Listicles:**
- Engage meaningfully on others' content
- Create roundup posts quoting multiple authorities
- Build mutual admiration through genuine engagement

**Quality standards:**
- Only boost content that has proven organic engagement
- Every promoted piece tracked in Content Library
- Dollar-a-Day phases strictly followed (don't skip testing)

**Human required:** Budget approval for scaling. Gift personalization. Relationship management.

## Stage 5: Perform (MAA)

**Purpose:** Measure, analyze, and optimize everything using the MAA framework.

**MAA cycle:**
1. **Metrics:** Pull actual numbers from native platforms. Week-over-week comparisons. Key metrics per ACC funnel stage.
2. **Analysis:** WHY are the numbers what they are? Connect cause to effect. Call out weakest area. NOT a task list.
3. **Action:** Specific next steps with owner + deadline. At least one action targets weakest metric. Minimum 3 items.

**Reporting cadence:**
- EOD reports daily from all team members
- MAA reports weekly (Fridays) posted to Basecamp Updates thread
- Monthly reporting with trend analysis

**Standards of Excellence:**
- Use to identify "hot" content (4 signals for when a video is hot)
- Score content on 30-point authority scale (What × Who × Where)
- Feed winners back into Process stage for repurposing

**The continuous loop:** Perform feeds back into Process. Winners get repurposed. Losers get analyzed. The cycle compounds.

**Human required:** Analysis requires human judgment. Dennis reviews and pushes back on poor quality. Strategic pivots are human decisions.

## Content Library

The Content Library is the single most important tracking tool:
- Google Sheet tracking ALL content assets
- Categories: social posts, blogs, videos, mentions, reviews, endorsements
- Scored on 3 Components of Authority (What, Who, Where) — 30-point scale
- Identifies "Greatest Hits" for evergreen promotion
- Prevents duplicate content creation
- Every team member, even freelancers, needs access

**"Power comes from other people's words about you, not from what you say about yourself."**

## Content Pyramid

Three layers of content production:
1. **Pillar Content** (top) — original long-form video, interview, or recording
2. **Micro Content** (middle) — repurposed articles, clips, posts, infographics
3. **Distribution** (bottom) — cross-posted across all channels, identifies Greatest Hits

Processing priority levels:
- **Low:** Simple post to blog + cross-post to socials
- **Medium:** Descript transcription + snippets + cross-post + $1/day boost for 20 days
- **High:** Careful editing + snippets + course potential + personal socials + email promotion

## E-E-A-T Compliance

Every piece of content must demonstrate:
- **Experience:** Real photos, geotagged media, personal stories
- **Expertise:** Demonstrated knowledge from doing, not just reading
- **Authority:** Third-party mentions, endorsed by others, published on high-authority properties
- **Trust:** Consistent information across all platforms, verified by Google's Knowledge Graph

**"AI doesn't have your stories because it's not human. When you're able to tell stories about your experiences, Google can recognize that your content is authentic and deserves to rank."**
