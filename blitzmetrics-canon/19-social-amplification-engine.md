# Social Amplification Engine (SAE) — [Methodology Partner] Canon

**Source:** Master Operations Process Guide (v12.5), Dollar-a-Day Presentation (v1.4), DAD Webinar Deck (2023)
**Version:** 1.0 | **Extracted:** 2026-04-01

---

## Overview

The Social Amplification Engine is the 6-phase master framework for client delivery. It is the overarching system that Operations runs every client through. Dollar-a-Day is Step 5 (Amplification) within the SAE. The Content Factory is the content production system that feeds the SAE.

**Key distinction:** The SAE is the **delivery framework** (how we run clients). The Content Factory is the **content production system** (how we create content). They work together but serve different purposes.

"The idea is to maximize what's already working for our clients -- to increase conversion rates on existing traffic and find more customers that look like their best customers. It's not for brand new products or businesses that don't have an existing funnel."

## Phase 1: Plumbing

Set up tracking on all digital ads platforms BEFORE spending money.

### Checklist

- [ ] Create Facebook Ads account via Business Manager
- [ ] Create Google Ads account tied to MCC
- [ ] Create Google Analytics (GA4) account
- [ ] Create Google Tag Manager account
- [ ] Publish GTM container and tags to website
- [ ] Facebook Instant Articles setup
- [ ] Google AMP setup
- [ ] Ensure pixels fire correctly on all key pages
- [ ] Verify Meta Pixel standard events
- [ ] Confirm call tracking is installed and routing correctly

### Quality Gate
No ad spend until every tracking element fires correctly. Verify with Tag Assistant and Facebook Pixel Helper.

---

## Phase 2: Goals

Determine goals for the next 90 days with ads.

### Checklist

- [ ] Define mission (start with WHY) and identify desired outcome + customer segments
- [ ] Identify primary goal(s) for next 90 days
- [ ] Determine target CPA or ROAS
- [ ] Determine ad budget relative to campaign goals (clicks, page likes, form submissions)
- [ ] Choose one key metric for each ACC funnel stage: Awareness, Consideration, Conversion
- [ ] Develop brand through 6 phases of Personal Branding
- [ ] Complete GCT discovery for the engagement

### Goal Examples
- Leads for agency
- Leads for webinar
- Leads for real estate
- Sales for offer
- Booked calls

### Quality Gate
GCT must be defined and documented before proceeding. "If you don't understand the GCT of you or your client, this guide will be useless."

---

## Phase 3: Content

Find or create content. Establish credibility while moving toward conversions.

### Checklist

- [ ] Assemble third-party endorsements from high-authority sites
- [ ] Create a WHY video (3-minute version using the WHY Video formula)
- [ ] Create 3x3 Video Grid (3 WHY, 3 HOW, 3 WHAT videos)
- [ ] Set up Content Library (Google Sheet tracking all assets)
- [ ] Map out one-minute videos per Topic Wheel topic
- [ ] Create promotional content to drive conversions
- [ ] Determine the ONE QUESTION that defines your expertise
- [ ] Gather content by ACC funnel stage, addressing key objections for each persona

### Quality Gate
Content Library populated with minimum 9 videos (3x3 grid) and tagged by Topic Wheel position and ACC stage.

---

## Phase 4: Targeting

Upload lists and customer data. Ad platforms are data machines -- the more data you feed initially, the better performance.

### Checklist

- [ ] Import customer and lead emails into Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, and Google as custom audiences
- [ ] Build targets on Facebook and Twitter:
  - Direct interests
  - Closest competitors
  - Common interests customers share
  - Industry influencers customers/competitors follow
  - People working in media
- [ ] Create 1% lookalike audience for each major landing page, thank you page, and email list
- [ ] Amplify a video and create video remarketing audiences (15+ second viewers)
- [ ] Create 1-day, 30-day, and 180-day Website Custom Audiences:
  - Site-wide
  - For each major landing page
  - For each thank you page
- [ ] Build funnel sequences with proper exclusions

### Quality Gate
Custom audiences built and verified. Lookalike audiences created. Funnel exclusions in place so users only see content appropriate to their stage.

## Phase 5: Amplification (Dollar-a-Day)

This is where Dollar-a-Day lives within the SAE.

### Checklist

- [ ] Boost top 3-5 Facebook posts to at least one saved audience per persona
- [ ] Execute 4-stage boost optimization:
  1. $1/day test across 5-10 pieces of content
  2. Kill underperformers after 7 days
  3. Scale winners to $5-20/day
  4. Graduate proven winners to evergreen rotation
- [ ] Set up remarketing ads for 1-day landing page abandoners on Google Ads and Facebook
- [ ] Create media inception ads using Dollar-a-Day strategy
- [ ] Add UTM parameters to every unpublished post URL
- [ ] Create unpublished posts and boost for website clicks and conversions

### 3x3 Video Grid Sequencing
- **Ad Set 1:** WHY videos to lookalike audiences, EXCLUDING WHAT video viewers
- **Ad Set 2:** HOW videos, EXCLUDING viewers who watched WHAT content
- **Ad Set 3:** WHAT videos with no exclusions
- **Ad Set 4:** Continued WHAT videos until conversion

### Performance Benchmarks
- Target $0.02 per video view
- Target $3 per lead
- Target $10 per conversion
- 10% engagement rate = winner

### Quality Gate
7-day testing window documented. Kill/keep/test decisions logged. UTM parameters verified on all posts.

## Phase 6: Optimization

Use data from ad platforms to adjust or scale up quickly and efficiently.

### Checklist

- [ ] Apply Metrics Decomposition to identify drivers
- [ ] Compare week-over-week performance
- [ ] Review budget allocation by channel (watch for statistical noise in small samples)
- [ ] List 3-5 recommendations weekly via [[blitzmetrics-canon/05-maa-framework|MAA]]
- [ ] Apply Top N analysis (focus resources on top performers)
- [ ] Refine lookalike audiences based on converter data
- [ ] Replicate winning campaigns (Content x Target) across channels:
  - Facebook (vertical video, custom audience/interests/untargeted)
  - Twitter/X (any aspect ratio, specific handles)
  - TikTok (any aspect ratio, specific companies/organizations)
  - LinkedIn (professional content, job title/employer targeting)
  - YouTube (pre-roll/in-feed, keywords/placements/custom intent)

### Cross-Channel Replication Rule
Once a winning Content x Target combination is found, replicate across platforms. Adjust format and targeting for each platform but keep the winning message the same.

**Critical Rule:** Do not exceed 10% of available audience daily. Exceeding this threshold causes audience burnout.

### Quality Gate
Weekly MAA reports produced. Budget reallocated based on data. Underperforming channels paused or adjusted.

## SAE vs Content Factory: How They Connect

| SAE Phase | Content Factory Stage | Relationship |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------|
| 1. Plumbing | Plumbing (Stage 0) | Same work -- foundation setup |
| 2. Goals | GCT Discovery | Goals define what content to produce |
| 3. Content | Produce + Process | CF creates the content SAE needs |
| 4. Targeting | -- | SAE-specific (audience building) |
| 5. Amplification | Promote | Dollar-a-Day + Thank You Machine |
| 6. Optimization | Perform/MAA | Same work -- measure and improve |

The Content Factory is the engine that produces content. The SAE is the vehicle that delivers results to clients. You cannot run one without the other.

---

## Client Qualification

Before onboarding a client into the SAE, they must have:

- An existing product or service with proven sales
- Happy customers who can provide testimonials
- Willingness to appear on camera or provide real content
- An existing funnel (even a basic one)
- Budget for at least 90 days of engagement

The SAE is NOT for:
- Brand new products with no market validation
- Businesses with no existing customers
- People unwilling to create authentic content

---

## Human Requirements

1. **GCT discovery requires human conversation** -- goals and targeting cannot be extracted by AI alone
2. **Content production requires the client** -- real videos, real stories, real endorsements
3. **Audience strategy requires human judgment** -- competitor selection, influencer identification
4. **Weekly optimization reviews require human analysis** -- statistical noise vs real signal
5. **Client communication** -- weekly recommendations must be delivered by a human who understands the relationship
