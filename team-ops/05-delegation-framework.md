## The Handoff Protocol

**This is the part that fixes the bottleneck.** I have a habit of forwarding emails to {{OWNER_NAME}}son with minimal context. That's lazy delegation and it creates confusion, rework, and delays. Here's how it works now:

### Step 1: Brief in Basecamp (NOT Email)

Every task goes in Basecamp as a to-do assigned to {{OWNER_NAME}}son (or whoever owns it). Not an email forward. Not a text. Basecamp.

**Why:** Email forwards die in inboxes. Basecamp tasks are tracked, visible to the whole team, and have comment threads. {{ADVISOR_NAME}} has been saying this. He's right.

### Step 2: Every Brief Includes These Five Things

```
1. WHAT'S NEEDED
   [Clear description of the deliverable. Not "handle this" — actual specifics.]

2. DEADLINE
   [Specific date and time. "ASAP" is not a deadline.]

3. QUALITY STANDARD
   [Link to the relevant QA doc or example of what "done" looks like.
    For articles: link to Article QA SOP.
    For design: link to an approved example.]

4. CONTEXT
   [Why this matters. Who it's for. What it connects to.]

5. WHO TO ASK IF STUCK
   [Not always me. Could be {{ADVISOR_NAME}} for content quality, {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}} for process,
    {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}} for tool access, Josh for technical questions.]
```

### Step 3: {{OWNER_NAME}}son Reviews {{ADVISOR_NAME}}'s Feedback Patterns

{{OWNER_NAME}}son needs to internalize the quality bar. That means:

- Reading {{ADVISOR_NAME}}'s feedback on past content (it's all in Basecamp and email threads)
- Understanding the common flags: AI-sounding language, missing specificity, lack of personal voice
- Reviewing the Article QA & Compliance SOP before every writing task
- When in doubt, asking {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}} to gut-check before submitting to me for final QA

### Step 4: I Review Output, Not Process

I don't need to know how {{OWNER_NAME}}son made it. I need to see the finished product and confirm it meets the quality bar. If it does, it ships. If it doesn't, I give specific feedback (not "make it better" — actual notes on what to fix).

## The Email Forwarding Problem — Solved

**Old way:**
1. I get an email about something
2. I forward it to {{OWNER_NAME}}son with "can you handle this"
3. No one else sees it
4. No deadline
5. No quality standard
6. {{OWNER_NAME}}son guesses what I want
7. I end up redoing half of it

**New way:**
1. I get an email about something
2. I create a Basecamp to-do with the five-part brief
3. I assign it to {{OWNER_NAME}}son (or whoever owns it)
4. I attach the email or relevant content as context
5. The whole team can see it
6. {{OWNER_NAME}}son knows exactly what's needed and when
7. It comes back right the first time

**Time saved:** 5 minutes to write a proper brief now saves 30+ minutes of back-and-forth and rework later.

## Weekly Self-Audit

Every Friday, I check myself against this framework. Five questions:

1. **Did I do anything on the "delegate" list this week?** If yes, why? Fix the handoff so it doesn't happen again.
2. **Did I forward emails to {{OWNER_NAME}}son instead of using Basecamp?** Count them. The number should be zero.
3. **How many hours did I spend on client-facing work vs. admin?** Target: 80% client-facing, 20% everything else.
4. **Did any client deliverable go out late?** If yes, was it because I was the bottleneck on QA?
5. **What did I do this week that {{ADVISOR_NAME}} would call "alluring but not important"?** Be honest.
