# Project Management SOP — How a Knowledge Panel Sprint Moves Through the System

## Purpose

This is the master workflow for every KP Sprint at {{COMPANY}}. Six stages, clear owners, hard deadlines. If you don't know where a project stands or who's responsible, this document answers it.

---

## Stage 1: Sales ({{OWNER_NAME}} / {{ADVISOR_NAME}})

**Owner:** {{OWNER_NAME}} (R), {{ADVISOR_NAME}} (C)

{{OWNER_NAME}} or {{ADVISOR_NAME}} sources the lead. Before anyone spends time on a discovery call, the lead must pass the qualification gate:

- **LSA category** — Must operate in a Local Services Ads-eligible vertical
- **$100k+/mo revenue** — We don't Sprint for businesses that can't afford to compound
- **100+ 5-star reviews** — Existing social proof means the Sprint has raw material to work with
- **Willing to film** — No film, no Content Factory. Non-negotiable.

If the lead passes qualification, send the scheduling link for a discovery call. No manual back-and-forth on times. Use the link.

---

## Stage 2: Intake (Day 0–2)

**Owner:** {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}} (R), {{OWNER_NAME}} (A)

The clock starts the moment the deal closes. {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}} has 48 hours to get the project scaffolded.

1. Create Basecamp project from the Sprint template
2. Assign RACI for every deliverable in the project
3. Send the **intake checklist** to the client — this includes:
   - Brand assets (logos, colors, fonts, headshots)
   - Bios and professional background
   - Account credentials (Google Business Profile, social accounts, domain registrar)
   - Existing content worth referencing
4. Confirm the interview date — get it on the calendar within the first week
5. Collect **50% upfront payment** before any production begins

**Gate:** No payment, no production start. No exceptions.

---

## Stage 3: Production (Day 3–20)

**Owner:** {{OWNER_NAME}} (R for PM), delivery labor pool executes

This is where the Sprint deliverables get built. Every item has an owner and a Basecamp task.

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Site scaffold** | Templated theme with full schema markup. No custom designs inside a Sprint. |
| **60-min recorded interview** | One session. Structured questions. This is the source of truth for all content. |
| **3 authority articles** | Written from transcript. Edited for clarity. SEO-optimized with target keywords. |
| **6 short-form videos** | Subtitled, branded, hooks + CTAs. Cut from the interview footage. |
| **10–20 citations** | Cleaned and published across relevant directories and platforms. |
| **Profile unification** | All profiles consistent — name, bio, headshot, links, NAP data. |

### Production Rules

- Every team member working on this project posts an **EOD report** in Basecamp
- Progress is tracked against the Day 30 deadline — not vibes, not "almost done," actual deliverable completion
- If you're blocked, say it immediately. Don't wait for the Tuesday standup.
- No custom work inside the Sprint. If the client asks for something outside scope, the answer is: *"Absolutely — as a Phase 2 engagement after the Sprint."*

---

## Stage 4: QA (Day 21–26)

**Owner:** {{ADVISOR_NAME}} (R), {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}} (C)

Every deliverable gets reviewed before it touches the client. This is not a rubber stamp — it's a real quality check.

1. **Technical QA** — Site loads correctly, schema validates, citations are live and accurate, videos play properly
2. **Content QA** — Articles are factually correct, SEO'd, on-brand, and free of AI slop
3. **Distribution plan finalized** — Where every asset gets published and when
4. **Dollar-a-Day starter plan** — Budget allocation, audience targeting, creative selection for the first $1/day campaigns
5. **Full deliverable review** — Every item checked against the Sprint checklist. If it's not on the list, it doesn't ship. If it is on the list and it's not done, the Sprint isn't done.

**Gate:** No QA sign-off, no client delivery.

---

## Stage 5: Delivery (Day 27–30)

**Owner:** {{OWNER_NAME}} (R)

This is the finish line. Make it clean.

1. **Client review** — Present all deliverables. Walk through what was built and why.
2. **Fixes applied** — Client feedback gets addressed. Reasonable revisions only — scope stays fixed.
3. **Entity Health Snapshot** — Before/after comparison of the client's digital presence. This is the proof that the Sprint worked.
4. **Next-step plan delivered** — Phase 2 recommendations, ongoing retainer options, or specific follow-up actions the client can take.
5. **50% balance collected** — Final payment before project closes. {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}} handles invoicing.

---

## Stage 6: Case Study

**Owner:** {{OWNER_NAME}} (R), {{ADVISOR_NAME}} (C)

**This is not optional.** If the case study isn't published, the Sprint isn't done. Period.

Every Sprint produces a case study that includes:

- Client background and starting position
- What was delivered
- Before/after metrics (Entity Health Snapshot data)
- Client testimonial or quote
- Published on the {{COMPANY}} site and distributed through our channels

The case study is the fuel for the next sale. Skip it and you're burning revenue.

## Status Tracking

Every project gets a **Red / Amber / Green** status against its Day 30 deadline:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Green** | On track. All deliverables progressing on schedule. |
| **Amber** | At risk. One or more deliverables behind. Recovery plan needed. |
| **Red** | Off track. Deadline will be missed without intervention. Escalate immediately. |

Status is reported in the **Tuesday standup**. No surprises. If a project flips from Green to Red between standups, that's a communication failure, not a delivery failure.

---

## Blocker Resolution

- **Document delays immediately.** The moment something is blocked, it goes in Basecamp with a description of what's blocked, why, and what's needed to unblock it.
- **Client delays pause the clock.** If the client hasn't delivered prerequisites (assets, access, interview availability), the 30-day clock pauses. Document this in Basecamp with written notice to the client.
- **Internal delays don't pause the clock.** If we're the bottleneck, that's on us. Fix it.

---

## Quality Checks

Before marking any Sprint complete, verify:

- [ ] All 6 deliverable categories completed and QA'd
- [ ] Entity Health Snapshot generated with before/after data
- [ ] Case study drafted and scheduled for publication
- [ ] Final payment collected
- [ ] Client has received the next-step plan
- [ ] All Basecamp tasks closed out

---

## Common Pitfalls

- **Starting production before payment clears.** Don't. 50% upfront means 50% upfront.
- **Letting scope creep in during production.** The Sprint is the Sprint. Everything else is Phase 2.
- **Waiting until Day 25 to start QA.** QA starts Day 21. Build the buffer. You'll need it.
- **Skipping the case study.** It feels like extra work after delivery. It's not extra — it's the whole point. The case study sells the next Sprint.
- **Not documenting client delays in writing.** Verbal agreements about paused clocks don't hold up. Put it in Basecamp.
