# {{COMPANY_NAME}} — Meeting Cadence

Every recurring meeting at {{COMPANY}} has a purpose, an agenda, and a clear output. If a meeting doesn't produce action items, it shouldn't exist. This document defines every standing meeting, who's in the room, what gets covered, and what happens after.

No meeting is optional for required attendees unless you've cleared it with {{OWNER_NAME}} in advance. If you can't make it, post your update in the Basecamp thread before the meeting starts.

---

## 1. Weekly Pipeline & Delivery Standup

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day/Time** | Tuesdays, 10:00 AM CST |
| **Duration** | 30 minutes (hard stop) |
| **Frequency** | Weekly |
| **Location** | Zoom (standing link in Basecamp) |

**Required Attendees:** {{OWNER_NAME}}, {{ADVISOR_NAME}}, {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}, Sam, {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}}
**Optional:** {{OWNER_NAME}}son (joins when he has active delivery items)

**Agenda Template:**

1. **Pipeline update** ({{OWNER_NAME}}, 5 min) — New leads this week, proposals sent, deals closed or lost. Numbers only, no storytelling.
2. **Delivery status** ({{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}, 10 min) — Red/Amber/Green for every active project against its Day 30 deadline. Red means behind and needs intervention. Amber means at risk. Green means on track.
3. **Blockers** (All, 5 min) — Anything stopping progress. Name the blocker, name who can unblock it, set a deadline.
4. **Financial snapshot** ({{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}}, 5 min) — Outstanding invoices, payments received this week, upcoming payouts.
5. **Priorities for the week** ({{OWNER_NAME}}, 5 min) — Top 3 priorities across the company. Everyone leaves knowing what matters most.

**Pre-Meeting Prep:**

- {{OWNER_NAME}}: Update pipeline tracker in Basecamp before the meeting. Know your numbers cold.
- {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}: Update project status (R/A/G) in Basecamp by Monday EOD.
- {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}}: Have current AR/AP numbers ready.
- Everyone: If you have a blocker, post it in the Basecamp thread before the meeting so we're not hearing it for the first time live.

**Post-Meeting Action Items:**

- {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}} posts meeting notes in the **{{COMPANY}} Weekly Standup** Basecamp thread within 1 hour.
- Format: Bulleted list of decisions made and action items assigned. Each action item includes owner and deadline.
- Example: `[ ] {{OWNER_NAME}} — Send proposal to [Client] by Wednesday 3/20`

**Notes Stored:** Basecamp > {{COMPANY}} Internal > Weekly Standup thread

| **Duration** | 45 minutes |
| **Frequency** | Monthly |
| **Location** | Zoom |

**Required Attendees:** {{OWNER_NAME}}, {{ADVISOR_NAME}}, {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}
**Optional:** {{OPS_ADMIN_NAME}} (joins for AR/AP section)

**Agenda Template:**

4. **Cash position and runway** ({{OWNER_NAME}}, 5 min) — Cash on hand. Outstanding receivables. Upcoming known expenses.

- {{ADVISOR_NAME}}: Come with specific questions or concerns. This is his accountability meeting — bring the hard questions.

**Post-Meeting Action Items:**

- {{OWNER_NAME}} posts the monthly P&L summary in Basecamp within 48 hours.
- Format: Revenue, expenses, net, margin, cash position. One paragraph of commentary on what drove the numbers.
- Any financial action items (chase invoice, cut expense, adjust pricing) assigned with owner and deadline.

**Notes Stored:** Basecamp > {{COMPANY}} Internal > Financial Reviews thread

**Why This Exists:** {{ADVISOR_NAME}} has been pushing for P&L accountability, and he's right. You can't run a real business without knowing your numbers monthly. This meeting forces the discipline.

## 5. Sprint Retro

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day/Time** | Scheduled within 3 business days of Sprint delivery |
| **Duration** | 30 minutes |
| **Frequency** | After every KP Sprint delivery |
| **Location** | Zoom |

**Required Attendees:** {{OWNER_NAME}}, {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}, and whoever executed the Sprint
**Optional:** {{ADVISOR_NAME}} (joins when available)

**Agenda Template:**

1. **Delivery scorecard** ({{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}, 5 min) — Did we hit Day 30? What was the final quality grade? Did anything get delivered late or below standard?
2. **What went well** (All, 5 min) — Specific things that worked. Name them so we can repeat them.
3. **What broke or was harder than it should have been** (All, 10 min) — Be honest. If a process sucked, say so. If a tool failed, name it. If communication broke down, identify where.
4. **Process improvements** (All, 5 min) — Based on what broke, what do we change for the next Sprint? Be specific — "communicate better" is not an improvement. "Post daily updates in the Basecamp thread by 5 PM CST" is.
5. **Case study status** ({{OWNER_NAME}}, 5 min) — Is the case study drafted? What's needed to finish it? Assign owner and deadline.

**Pre-Meeting Prep:**

- {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}}: Pull the Sprint timeline. Note any missed deadlines, quality flags, or scope issues.
- {{OWNER_NAME}}: Review the final deliverables. Have the client's feedback (or lack thereof) ready.
- Team: Think about what you'd do differently. Come with at least one specific suggestion.

**Post-Meeting Action Items:**

- {{OPS_PARTNER_NAME}} posts the retro summary in the specific client's Basecamp project.
- Format: What went well (bullets), what to improve (bullets), process changes for next Sprint (bullets with owners).
- Any process changes get added to the KP Sprint Playbook ([04-kp-sprint-playbook.md](./04-kp-sprint-playbook.md)) within one week.

**Notes Stored:** Basecamp > [Client Project] > Sprint Retro thread

## Meeting Rules

These apply to every meeting listed above.

1. **Start on time.** If you're not there when it starts, we start without you.
2. **Come prepared.** Every meeting has pre-work. If you didn't do the pre-work, you're wasting everyone else's time.
3. **No status updates that could have been a Basecamp post.** Meetings are for decisions, blockers, and alignment — not reading updates aloud.
4. **Action items have owners and deadlines.** "We should do X" is not an action item. "{{OWNER_NAME}} will do X by Friday" is.
5. **Notes get posted in Basecamp the same day.** If it's not in Basecamp, it didn't happen.
6. **If a meeting consistently has no agenda items, cancel it.** Don't meet for the sake of meeting. But don't skip accountability meetings just because things feel fine — that's usually when things are about to break.
7. **Cameras on.** Especially for 1:1s and retros. It matters.
