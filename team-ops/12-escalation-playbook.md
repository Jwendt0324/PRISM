# 12 — Escalation Playbook

**What to do when things break at [Your Agency].**

Every business hits problems. The difference is whether you have a system for handling them or you're scrambling every time. Reference [[memory-bank/04-client-directory|Client Directory]] for client contact details during escalations. This is the system.

---

## 1. Client Is Unhappy

**Response time:** Same business day.

1. **Acknowledge immediately in Basecamp.** Not email, not text — Basecamp, so the whole team has visibility. Simple: "We hear you, we're on it."
2. **Loop [Your Mentor/Advisor] if it's a quality or scope issue.** [Your Mentor/Advisor] leads quality. If the client is unhappy about the work itself, he needs to know within hours, not days.
3. **Propose resolution within 24 hours.** Concrete next steps, not vague promises. "We'll have a revised version by Thursday" beats "We'll look into it."
4. **Document root cause.** What actually went wrong? Was it a process failure, a communication gap, or a mismatched expectation? Log it so we fix the system, not just the symptom.

---

## 2. Deliverable Is Late

**Response time:** Immediately upon realizing the deadline will be missed.

1. **Notify [Your Name] + [Your Mentor/Advisor] immediately.** No one should find out a deliverable is late by checking a due date. Proactive communication always.
2. **Update red/amber/green status** in the project tracker.
3. **If client delay caused it:** Document the delay with dates and specifics. Pause the clock with written notice to the client. "We're pausing the Sprint timeline as of [date] pending [deliverable] from your team."
4. **If team delay caused it:** Identify the specific blocker. Reassign if someone is stuck or overloaded. Update the timeline and communicate the new date to the client.

---

## 3. Team Member Is Unresponsive

**Response time:** Escalate after 24 hours of silence.

1. **Text or call directly.** Don't just send another email into the void.
2. **If no response within 24 hours, escalate to [Your Name].**
3. **If [Your Name] is unresponsive, escalate to [Your Mentor/Advisor].**
4. **Document the gap.** If someone is consistently unreachable, that's a pattern worth addressing in the next 1:1.

---

## 4. Scope Creep Request

**Response:** Immediate redirect. No exceptions.

The line: **"Great idea — let's add that to Phase 2 after we deliver the Sprint."**

Do not negotiate scope mid-Sprint. Do not add "one more thing." The Sprint is the Sprint. Additional requests get documented in Basecamp as a future phase item. If the client pushes, [Your Name] handles the conversation.

---

## 5. Payment Dispute

**Response time:** Within 48 hours.

1. **[Your Ops Admin] handles the initial inquiry.** He pulls up payment records, contract terms, and timeline.
2. **If unresolved, escalate to [Your Name] + [Your Mentor/Advisor].** They decide together.
3. **7-day cooling off period** per the client agreement. If a client requests a refund within 7 days of payment and before assignments have started, process it cleanly.
4. **Performance-based grace:** If no assignments have been started or delivered, the cooling off period applies. Once work has begun, evaluate on a case-by-case basis.

---

## 6. Quality Issue Found Post-Delivery

**Response time:** Same business day.

1. **[Your Mentor/Advisor] leads remediation.** He reviews the issue, determines severity, and directs the fix.
2. **Document what went wrong.** Was it a missed QA step? An unclear brief? A capability gap?
3. **Update the QA checklist** to prevent recurrence. Every quality failure should result in a new checkpoint in the review process.
4. **Communicate with the client.** Own it, fix it fast, show what changed so it won't happen again.

---

## 7. Client Wants a Refund

**Response time:** Within 48 hours of request.

1. **7-day cooling off policy.** If requested within 7 days of payment and no work has started, process the refund. No friction.
2. **After 7 days:** Evaluate on a case-by-case basis. Consider what was delivered, what was promised, and whether the issue is fixable.
3. **[Your Name] + [Your Mentor/Advisor] decide together.** Neither approves a refund solo.
4. **[Your Ops Admin] processes** once approved.
5. **Document the reason.** Refund requests are feedback. What drove the request? Fix the root cause.

---

## 8. AI Language Detected in Content

**Response time:** Before publishing. Period.

1. **Flag immediately.** If anyone on the team spots AI-sounding language in a deliverable, it gets pulled.
2. **Revise before publishing.** No exceptions. AI-sounding content does not go to clients or get published.
3. **[Your Mentor/Advisor]'s standard applies:** "Write like a journalist, not a bot." Real quotes, specific details, human rhythm.
4. **If it shipped already:** Pull it down, revise, republish. Notify the client with the updated version.

---

## 9. Tool Access Emergency

**Response time:** ASAP.

1. **Contact [Your Ops Admin] via email.** He maintains the master access registry.
2. **Time zone advantage:** [Your Ops Admin] is in Pakistan (UTC+5). If it's overnight US time, he's likely available and can respond quickly.
3. **If [Your Ops Admin] is unavailable:** Check the Tool Stack document (09) for admin credentials and backup access info.
4. **If no one can resolve:** Escalate to [Your Name], who escalates to [Your Mentor/Advisor] if it involves [Methodology Partner]/[Partner Platform] shared tools.

---

## 10. Budget / Expense Dispute

**Response time:** Within 1 week.

1. **[Your Name] + [Your Mentor/Advisor] review together.** No unilateral decisions on money.
2. **Revenue flows to [Your Name] first, then distributed per the equity agreement.** This is the documented structure.
3. **[Your Ops Admin] provides supporting documentation** (invoices, receipts, P&L entries).
4. **If it involves [Partner Platform]/[Methodology Partner] shared costs:** [Your Mentor/Advisor] leads resolution. [Your Agency] should not be subsidizing [Partner Platform] infrastructure that [Your Agency] can't reliably access.

---

## General Principles

- **Speed matters.** Most escalations get worse with time. Respond fast, even if the full solution takes longer.
- **Document everything in Basecamp.** Email escalations create silos. Basecamp keeps the whole team informed.
- **Fix systems, not symptoms.** Every escalation should result in a process improvement, not just a one-time fix.
- **No blame, just solutions.** We're a small team. Pointing fingers wastes time. Find the fix, implement it, move on.
