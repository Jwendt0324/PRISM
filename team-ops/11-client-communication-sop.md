# Client Communication SOP

## Purpose

Every client touchpoint follows a pattern per [[memory-bank/08-communication-patterns|Communication Patterns]]. This SOP defines what gets said, by whom, and through which channel at every stage of a KP Sprint engagement. No improvising. No orphaned threads. No confusion about who's talking to the client.

---

## Stage 1: Initial Outreach / Qualification

**Owner:** [Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor]

[Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor] initiates contact with qualified leads. This is not a team activity — only [Your Name] or [Your Mentor/Advisor] makes first contact.

- Use the **scheduling link** for discovery calls. No manual time coordination.
- Qualification happens before the call, not during it. Don't waste a call on a lead that doesn't pass the gate (LSA category, $100k+/mo revenue, 100+ reviews, willing to film).
- Keep the outreach direct. No pitch decks on first touch. The goal is to get them on a call, not to close via email.

---

## Stage 2: Proposal and Close

**Owner:** [Your Mentor/Advisor] (default closer), unless another closer is recorded

[Your Mentor/Advisor] handles proposals and closing unless [Your Name] or another team member is explicitly assigned as closer for a specific deal.

- Every closed deal must have **CRM evidence** — the deal record, proposal sent, and agreement signed. If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.
- Proposal format follows the standard Sprint scope. No custom proposals inside Sprint pricing.
- Once the deal closes, [Your Mentor/Advisor] (or the assigned closer) hands off to [Your Ops Admin] for intake. The handoff includes: client name, contact info, scope confirmed, payment terms, and any special notes from the sales conversation.

---

## Stage 3: Onboarding (Intake)

**Owner:** [Your Ops Admin]

[Your Ops Admin] sends the onboarding email within 24 hours of deal close. This is the client's first operational touchpoint with [Your Agency] — make it clean.

1. **Basecamp project created** from Sprint template with RACI assigned
2. **Client added to public threads** in Basecamp — they see project-level updates, task completions, and deliverable previews
3. **Onboarding email sent** with:
   - Welcome and what to expect over the next 30 days
   - Intake checklist (brand assets, bios, account access)
   - Interview scheduling link
   - Payment instructions for 50% upfront
4. **Basecamp URL shared** with the client so they can access their project directly

---

## Stage 4: Weekly Updates During Sprint

**Owner:** Whoever is R on the project (typically [Your Name] for PM)

Every Friday, post a weekly update in Basecamp using the **MAA format**:

### MAA Template

**Metrics** — What moved this week. Quantifiable where possible.
- Citations published: X
- Articles drafted/published: X
- Videos edited: X
- Site progress: [describe]

**Analysis** — What the metrics mean. Context, not just numbers.
- Are we on track for Day 30?
- Any quality concerns?
- Client responsiveness?

**Action** — What happens next week. Specific tasks, specific owners.
- [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
- [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]

Post the MAA in the **public Basecamp thread** so the client can see it. This is their window into progress. If they have to ask "what's happening with my project?" — we've already failed.

---

## Stage 5: Handling Client Requests

**Rule: Anything outside Sprint scope goes to the Phase 2 list. No exceptions.**

When a client asks for something that isn't in the Sprint deliverables:

1. Acknowledge the request — don't ignore it or dismiss it
2. Confirm it's outside the current Sprint scope
3. Add it to the **Phase 2 list** in Basecamp
4. Respond with: *"Great idea. We're adding that to your Phase 2 recommendations so it's captured and prioritized for after the Sprint."*

Do not negotiate scope mid-Sprint. Do not squeeze in "one small thing." The Sprint is fixed. This protects the client's timeline as much as ours.

---

## Stage 6: Delivery and Handoff

**Owner:** [Your Name]

When deliverables are ready (post-QA):

1. Schedule a **delivery walkthrough** — screen share, walk through every deliverable, explain what was built and why
2. Give the client time to review. Set a **5 business day review window** — after that, deliverables are considered accepted.
3. **Apply fixes** from client feedback. Reasonable revisions only. If they're asking for new work, see Stage 5.
4. Deliver the **Entity Health Snapshot** — before/after data. This is the proof of value.
5. Collect **final 50% payment** before officially closing the project.

---

## Stage 7: Post-Delivery Follow-Up

**Owner:** [Your Name] (R), [Your Mentor/Advisor] (C)

After delivery is complete:

1. **Case study drafted** — Start within one week of delivery. Include before/after metrics, client quote, and deliverable highlights. See [13-case-study-template.md](./13-case-study-template.md).
2. **Phase 2 proposal sent** (if applicable) — If the Phase 2 list has items worth pursuing, draft a proposal within two weeks of Sprint close.
3. **Client testimonial requested** — Ask for a written or video testimonial. Strike while the iron is hot — right after delivery when satisfaction is highest.
4. **30-day check-in** — One month post-delivery, check in on results. Knowledge Panels take time to populate — this is when early results start showing.

---

## Stage 8: When to Escalate to [Your Mentor/Advisor]

Escalate immediately — don't try to handle these solo:

- **Client is unhappy** — About deliverable quality, timeline, communication, anything. [Your Mentor/Advisor] gets looped in before it festers.
- **Scope disputes** — Client claims something was promised that isn't in the Sprint. [Your Mentor/Advisor] was on the sales call — he has context.
- **Quality concerns** — If QA flags something significant, [Your Mentor/Advisor] reviews before it goes to the client.
- **EEAT issues** — Anything related to Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness that could affect the client's digital presence or our methodology. [Your Mentor/Advisor] owns the methodology.

When escalating: send [Your Mentor/Advisor] a Basecamp message (not email, not text) with the project URL, a summary of the issue, and what you've tried so far.

---

## Communication Rules

These are non-negotiable. Violate them and you create chaos.

1. **Never @mention clients in Basecamp.** It creates duplicate email notifications and confuses the thread. Clients are on public threads — they'll see updates without being tagged.

2. **Use public threads for client-visible communication.** Project updates, deliverable previews, weekly MAAs, scheduling — anything the client should see goes in a public thread.

3. **Use private threads for internal communication.** Strategy discussions, QA feedback, blocker resolution, team coordination — anything the client should NOT see goes in a private thread.

4. **Always include the Basecamp URL when referencing project work.** In emails, in Slack, in any communication about a project — drop the Basecamp link. Don't make people search for context.

5. **Respond to client messages within one business day.** Even if the answer is "looking into this, will get back to you by [date]." Silence is not a status update.

6. **No project communication via text or personal channels.** If it's about the project, it goes in Basecamp. If it's a quick scheduling question, email is acceptable. Text messages create invisible threads that the team can't reference.

---

## Quality Checks

Before any client-facing communication goes out:

- [ ] Correct client name and project details
- [ ] Sent from the right channel (public vs private thread, email vs Basecamp)
- [ ] No @mentions of the client in Basecamp
- [ ] Basecamp URL included where relevant
- [ ] MAA format followed for weekly updates
- [ ] Tone is professional, direct, and confident — not overly casual, not corporate

---

## Common Pitfalls

- **@mentioning clients in Basecamp.** Everyone does it once. The duplicate notification email confuses the client and clutters their inbox. Use public threads — they'll see it.
- **Letting the client drive scope mid-Sprint.** Being responsive is good. Saying yes to everything is bad. Phase 2 list exists for a reason.
- **Sending updates without the MAA format.** Freeform updates are hard to parse and easy to misread. Metrics, Analysis, Action. Every time.
- **Escalating to [Your Mentor/Advisor] via email or text.** He's managing multiple projects. If it's not in Basecamp, it gets lost. Use Basecamp for escalations.
- **Forgetting the post-delivery follow-up.** The Sprint ends. Everyone moves on. Then we have no case study, no testimonial, and no Phase 2 proposal. Set a calendar reminder for the 30-day check-in.
