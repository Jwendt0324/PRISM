# Communication Protocol — [Your Agency Name]

This is how we communicate. No ambiguity. If you're unsure where something goes, default to Basecamp. Email gets lost. Basecamp doesn't.

---

## 1. Channel Definitions

### Basecamp — The Default

Everything about a client, a project, or a deliverable lives in Basecamp. Period.

**Use Basecamp for:**
- All project work and deliverable tracking
- Client-visible updates (public threads)
- Internal team discussion (private threads)
- EOD reports (daily)
- MAA reports (weekly)
- To-dos and task assignments
- Content Factory progress updates
- Dollar-a-Day campaign tracking
- Influence Report Card updates

**Rule:** If you're about to send an email about a project, stop. Put it in Basecamp instead. Email gets lost in a hurry.

### Email — 1:1 and External Only

Email is for direct coordination that doesn't belong in a project thread.

**Use email for:**
- Direct 1:1 coordination between team members
- External contacts (prospects, vendors, partners)
- Sensitive matters (compensation, legal, contracts)
- Tool access and credentials ([Your Ops Admin] handles onboarding assets)
- Forwarding tasks with context to specific people
- [Your Mentor/Advisor]'s strategic direction and high-level decisions

**Rule:** If you CC more than 2 people on an email about project work, it should be a Basecamp thread.

### Text / Phone — Emergencies Only

Do not text about project work. Do not call about deliverables.

**What counts as an emergency:**
- Client-facing deadline that will be missed in the next 2 hours
- System outage affecting live campaigns or client-visible assets
- Legal or PR issue requiring immediate response
- Travel logistics that change same-day plans

**What is NOT an emergency:**
- A question that can wait until tomorrow
- A deliverable that's behind schedule (put it in Basecamp)
- Something [Your Mentor/Advisor] asked about (he operates at all hours — match his energy in Basecamp, not via text)

### Zoom — Scheduled Meetings Only

- **Weekly standup:** Tuesdays, 30 minutes
- **Client interviews:** Scheduled as needed, always with 24-hour notice
- **AI Apprentice Office Hours:** Thursdays, 2:00 PM PST
- **Ad hoc calls:** Only when a Basecamp thread has gone 3+ rounds without resolution

**Rule:** No surprise Zoom calls. If it's not on the calendar, don't expect anyone to join.

## 4. Client Communication Rules

Clients interact with us through Basecamp. Keep it clean.

1. **All client communication goes through Basecamp.** Clients can see public threads. Write accordingly.
2. **Internal discussion goes in private threads.** Mark them "private" — team-only. Never assume a thread is private by default.
3. **Never @mention clients in Basecamp.** This creates duplicate email notifications and clutters their inbox. If a client needs to see something, post it in a public thread — they'll get the normal notification.
4. **Weekly MAA update to every active client, every Friday.** No exceptions. Format: Metrics, Analysis, Action. Even if there's not much to report, send the update. Silence kills client confidence.
5. **Respond to client messages same business day.** Even if the answer is "we're working on it and will have an update by [date]."
6. **No jargon in client-facing threads.** They don't care about our internal processes. They care about results.

## 5. Travel & Schedule Protection

From the KP Sprint playbook — these rules exist because burnout kills output.

1. **No same-day itinerary flips.** If the plan for today was set yesterday, it stays. Last-minute schedule changes destroy focus and create chaos. Push changes to tomorrow.
2. **Two protected sleep nights per week.** Minimum. Non-negotiable. No late-night calls, no "quick" tasks after 10 PM. Pick your two nights and defend them.
3. **Travel days are not work days.** If someone is traveling, don't expect deliverables. Plan around it.

## 6. Status Updates

### EOD Reports — Daily

- **Where:** Basecamp
- **When:** End of your business day
- **What:** What you worked on, what's done, what's blocked
- **Keep it short.** 3-5 bullet points. Not a novel.

### MAA Reports — Weekly (Fridays)

- **Where:** Basecamp (client-facing version in public thread, internal version in private thread)
- **Format:**
  - **Metrics:** Numbers. What moved this week.
  - **Analysis:** Why it moved (or didn't).
  - **Action:** What we're doing next week based on the data.

### Weekly Standup — Tuesdays, 30 Minutes

- **Where:** Zoom
- **Format:** Round-robin. Each person: what's done, what's next, what's blocked.
- **Rule:** If it takes more than 2 minutes to explain, take it offline into a Basecamp thread.
