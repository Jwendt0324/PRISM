# Knowledge Panel Sprint — Delivery SOP

## Purpose

This SOP defines exactly how we deliver a [[skills/kp-sprint|Knowledge Panel Sprint]]: what's included, what's not, who does what, how they're paid, and what "done" looks like. Claude should reference this for any Sprint-related work — client onboarding, project management, content production, QA, or team coordination.

## When to Use

- Starting a new Knowledge Panel Sprint engagement
- Onboarding a qualified DFY client
- Managing Sprint delivery timelines
- Assigning and tracking Sprint tasks
- Running QA passes on deliverables
- Calculating team compensation on a Sprint
- Creating case studies from completed Sprints

## The Product

Knowledge Panel Sprint — $6,000, 30 days, fixed scope, zero custom detours.

### Payment Terms
- 50% to start, 50% on delivery
- Clock pauses if client blocks prerequisites
- No custom work inside Sprint — extra wants = Phase 2 after delivery

### Qualification Gate
- LSA category business
- $100k+/mo revenue
- 100+ 5-star reviews
- Willing to film

## Fixed Deliverables

### 1. Personal Brand Site (live, templated theme)
- Entity-first architecture: Home, About, Media/Press, Speaking, Services, Contact
- schema.org Person/Organization + social/knowledge graph linking
- Citation hub with outbound links to verified profiles and press

### 2. Entity Foundations
- 10-20 citations/mentions cleaned and published (associations, press, directories)
- Unified NAP/handles + indexable profiles

### 3. Authority Content
- 1 recorded interview (60 min) → 3 authority articles (edited/SEO'd)
- 6 short-form videos (subtitled, branded, hooks + CTAs)

### 4. Distribution Mini-Plan
- Posting plan + [[blitzmetrics-canon/04-dollar-a-day|Dollar-a-Day]] starter plan and budget map

### 5. Entity Health Snapshot
- "Panel likelihood" score + checklist of next moves

## 30-Day Delivery Process

### Day 0-2: Intake & Prerequisites
- Send intake checklist to client (brand assets, bios, accounts)
- Collect all prerequisites
- Set up project in Basecamp/PM tool
- Confirm interview date

### Day 3-7: Foundation
- Site scaffold built on templated theme
- Schema markup implemented (Person/Organization)
- Interview scheduled and recorded (60 min)
- Begin citation research

### Day 8-20: Production
- 3 authority articles written from interview transcript (edited, SEO'd)
- 6 short-form videos produced (subtitled, branded, hooks + CTAs)
- 10-20 citations cleaned and published
- Profiles unified (NAP/handles)
- Internal linking completed

### Day 21-26: QA & Distribution Setup
- Technical QA passes on site, schema, citations
- Distribution plan finalized
- Dollar-a-Day starter plan and budget map created
- All deliverables reviewed against checklist

### Day 27-30: Handoff
- Client review of all deliverables
- Fixes applied
- Entity Health Snapshot generated
- Next-step plan delivered
- Case study drafted from engagement

### Client Must-Dos
- Provide brand assets, bios, account access on time
- Attend 60-min interview
- Approve copy quickly (delays pause the clock)

## Team Roles & Compensation (Per $6,000 Sprint)

Role-based. Paid on collected cash.

### Sales Commission (Closer): 15% — $900
- Paid to the person who sourced AND closed the deal
- Requires CRM evidence: lead source, call notes, proposal sent, signature
- [Your Mentor/Advisor] is default closer unless another closer is recorded

### Delivery Lead (PM): 10% — $600
- Paid on on-time delivery (<=30 days)
- If late due to documented client delay, still payable
- [Your AgentBuilder Partner] McLeod is default; assigned PM earns this if [Your AgentBuilder Partner] is not on the project

### Delivery Labor Pool: 35% — $2,100
- Budget for writers, editors, designers, VAs
- Paid by task per SOP rate card (per article, per clip, per citation block)
- [Your AgentBuilder Partner] allocates; [Your Mentor/Advisor] approves exceptions

### QA/On-Time Bonus Pool: 5% — $300
- Released ONLY if project ships on time with zero critical defects
- Split among team members who passed QA on their tasks

### Company Retained Margin: 35% — $2,100
- Covers ops, software, R&D ([Partner Platform]), runway

## BD KPI Gate ([Your Name] or anyone in BD role)

- Minimum activity: 10 qualified intros/week OR 4 closes/month AND >=70% show rate
- Miss two consecutive months → BD role paused (no commissions) until re-approved
- Vacation >7 consecutive business days → reassign owned opps

## Governance & Cadence

### Weekly Standup (Tuesdays, 30 min)
- Pipeline: new leads, proposals out, closes
- Delivery: red/amber/green per project vs. Day 30

### Scoreboard (public)
- Closes this week/month
- On-time delivery %
- Defect rate
- Client CSAT
- Case studies shipped

### Boundaries
- No new services, pilots, or detours for 90 days — requests go to Phase 2 list
- Every Sprint yields a published case study — if it's not published, it's not done
- SOPs and checklists must be followed for comp eligibility

## Quality Checks

- All 5 deliverable categories complete before handoff?
- Schema markup validates (test with Google Rich Results Test)?
- All citations live and indexable?
- Articles pass E-E-A-T standards?
- Short-form videos have subtitles, branding, hooks, and CTAs?
- Dollar-a-Day plan includes specific budget allocation and content selection?
- Entity Health Snapshot includes actionable next steps?
- Case study drafted and ready for publishing?
- Client signed off on all deliverables?
- CRM updated with final status?

## Common Pitfalls

- Accepting clients who don't meet qualification gate → enforce $100k+/mo, 100+ reviews, willing to film. Misfits go to [Partner Platform] $300 tier
- Scope creep inside the Sprint → all custom requests go to Phase 2 list. No exceptions
- Client delays killing the 30-day timeline → document delays immediately, pause clock with written notice
- Skipping the case study → "If it's not published, it's not done." Build case study creation into Day 27-30
- Paying comp without CRM evidence → require lead source, call notes, proposal, signature before releasing sales commission
- QA bonus released despite defects → zero critical defects means zero. Hold the standard

## Human Gates
| Step | Gate Type | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Qualification gate | Review | Human reviews revenue, review count, willingness to film — no auto-admit |
| Client interview (Day 3-7) | Execute | 60-minute recorded interview — client must participate in person |
| Client approval of copy (Day 21-26) | Approve | Client signs off on all written content before publishing |
| Technical QA passes (Day 21-26) | Review | Schema, citations, site validated by human |
| Client sign-off on all deliverables (Day 27-30) | Approve | Final handoff requires explicit client approval |
| [Your Mentor/Advisor] approves compensation exceptions | Approve | Any deviation from standard comp requires [Your Mentor/Advisor]'s sign-off |
| Case study publication | Execute | Every Sprint must produce a published case study — human writes and publishes |

## Anti-Vandalism Checks
- [ ] Qualification gate enforced ($100k+/mo, 100+ reviews, willing to film) — no exceptions
- [ ] Fixed scope maintained — all custom requests go to Phase 2 list
- [ ] Schema markup validates via Google Rich Results Test before handoff
- [ ] All citations are live and indexable before handoff
- [ ] CRM updated with final status and all evidence documented
- [ ] Case study drafted and ready for publishing (if not published, Sprint is not done)
