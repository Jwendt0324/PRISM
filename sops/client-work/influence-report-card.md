# Influence Report Card (MAA Model)

## Purpose
The Influence Report Card is a systematic assessment of a founder's public authority and presence across five key axes. It provides both baseline measurement and ongoing progress tracking within a 6-month strategic influence blueprint. The MAA (Metrics, Analysis, Action) model combines quantitative data with qualitative insights and prioritized action items.

## When to Use
- At project kickoff to establish baseline authority
- Monthly (15-minute check-ins) to track progress
- When pivoting strategy or adding new channels
- To demonstrate ROI to client and justify continued investment
- Before launching major campaigns (speaking, content, paid promotion)

## Process

### Phase 0: Baseline Assessment (Initial Setup)

**Step 1: Gather Current Data (Week 1)**
Create a baseline measurement across five axes. This takes 2-4 hours initially, then 15 minutes monthly.

**Axis 1: Web Presence & SEO Authority**
- Personal website traffic (GA4): monthly visitors, bounce rate, avg session duration
- Search rankings: research top 5 keywords client wants to rank for
  - Example for "growth marketing expert": rank for "growth marketing," "DTC growth strategy," "retention marketing," etc.
  - Use SEMrush or Ahrefs to get current ranking positions (record as "Position 1-10," "11-50," "51-100," "Not in top 100")
- Domain authority: Check Moz DA or Ahrefs DR score
- Backlinks: Count referring domains (quality over quantity)
- Google My Business: Review count, star rating, Q&A engagement
- Metrics to capture:
  - Monthly organic traffic
  - # keywords ranking top 10
  - # keywords ranking top 50
  - Domain Authority score
  - Referring domain count

**Axis 2: Public Speaking & Authority**
- Speaking engagements: Count in last 12 months (podcasts, conferences, webinars, workshops)
- Speaking platforms: Which venues? (size, quality, audience fit)
- Media mentions: Google Alerts, press coverage, industry publications
- Testimonials/social proof: Count testimonial videos, written endorsements
- Metrics to capture:
  - # speaking engagements (last 12 months)
  - # media mentions (last 12 months)
  - # testimonial videos
  - List of top 3 speaking platforms used

**Axis 3: Publishing & Content Authority**
- Blog/website: Monthly blog traffic, # posts published (last 12 months)
- Guest posts: # published on external sites, quality/domain authority
- LinkedIn: Follower count, avg post engagement rate, monthly impressions
- Medium/Substack: If applicable, follower count and engagement
- Bylines: Count of published articles with author credentials
- Metrics to capture:
  - # blog posts (last 12 months)
  - # guest posts (last 12 months)
  - Blog traffic per month
  - LinkedIn followers
  - LinkedIn avg engagement rate (likes + comments / followers)
  - LinkedIn monthly impressions

**Axis 4: Podcasting (Guest or Host)**
- Podcast appearances: Count of guest episodes (last 12 months), show quality
- Owned podcast: If applicable, # episodes, listener count, growth rate
- Podcast transcripts: Whether appearances are transcribed and used for SEO
- Metrics to capture:
  - # podcast guest appearances (last 12 months)
  - Estimated combined listener reach
  - # owned podcast episodes (if applicable)
  - Monthly downloads (if applicable)

**Axis 5: Social Media Authority**
- LinkedIn: Followers, post frequency, engagement rate
- Twitter/X: Followers, audience quality, retweet/reply rate
- Instagram: Followers, engagement rate, story/Reel performance
- TikTok: Followers, video completion rate, shares (if applicable)
- Facebook: Page followers, engagement, community strength
- Metrics to capture:
  - Follower count across platforms
  - Combined monthly reach/impressions
  - Average engagement rate by platform
  - # posts per week
  - Growth rate (3-month trend)

**Step 2: Create Grading Rubric (Week 1)**
Establish what "good" looks like for each axis based on industry benchmarks and client goals.

**Grading Scale: A, B, C, D, F**

**Web Presence Grading**
- **A (90-100)**: >1,000 monthly organic visitors, 5+ keywords top 10, DA >40, 50+ referring domains
- **B (80-89)**: 500-1,000 monthly visitors, 3-4 keywords top 10, DA 30-40, 25-50 referring domains
- **C (70-79)**: 200-500 monthly visitors, 1-2 keywords top 10, DA 20-30, 10-25 referring domains
- **D (60-69)**: <200 monthly visitors, 0-1 keyword top 10, DA <20, <10 referring domains
- **F (<60)**: No measurable organic traffic or authority

**Public Speaking Grading**
- **A (90-100)**: 12+ speaking engagements/year, includes 3+ major conferences, tier-1 media mentions monthly
- **B (80-89)**: 8-12 engagements/year, includes 1-2 major conferences, 2-3 media mentions/quarter
- **C (70-79)**: 4-7 engagements/year, mostly webinars/workshops, 1-2 media mentions/quarter
- **D (60-69)**: 1-3 engagements/year, mostly small platforms, no recent media
- **F (<60)**: 0 engagements in last 12 months

**Publishing Grading**
- **A (90-100)**: 2+ blog posts/month, 5+ guest posts/year on high-authority sites, 10K+ monthly blog traffic, LinkedIn 5K+ followers with 5%+ engagement
- **B (80-89)**: 1-2 posts/month, 3-4 guest posts/year, 2-5K monthly blog traffic, LinkedIn 2-5K followers with 3-5% engagement
- **C (70-79)**: 0.5-1 post/month, 1-2 guest posts/year, 500-2K monthly traffic, LinkedIn 500-2K followers with 1-3% engagement
- **D (60-69)**: Sporadic posting, 0 guest posts, <500 monthly traffic, LinkedIn <500 followers
- **F (<60)**: No blog, no publishing activity

**Podcasting Grading**
- **A (90-100)**: 12+ guest appearances/year on tier-1 podcasts, 50K+ combined listener reach, or owned podcast with 10K+ monthly downloads
- **B (80-89)**: 8-12 appearances/year on quality shows, 20-50K reach, or owned podcast 3-10K monthly downloads
- **C (70-79)**: 4-7 appearances/year on mid-tier shows, 5-20K reach, or owned podcast <3K monthly downloads
- **D (60-69)**: 1-3 appearances/year, <5K reach, no owned podcast
- **F (<60)**: No podcast activity

**Social Media Grading**
- **A (90-100)**: 10K+ combined followers, 5%+ average engagement rate, daily posting, 20%+ month-over-month growth
- **B (80-89)**: 5-10K followers, 3-5% engagement, 4-5x/week posting, 10-20% monthly growth
- **C (70-79)**: 2-5K followers, 1-3% engagement, 2-3x/week posting, 5-10% monthly growth
- **D (60-69)**: 500-2K followers, <1% engagement, sporadic posting, <5% growth
- **F (<60)**: <500 followers, no engagement, no activity

### Phase 1: Monthly Check-In (15-minute cadence)

**Step 1: Collect Data (5 minutes)**
- Pull analytics from GA4 (web traffic)
- Pull LinkedIn follower count and this month's engagement
- Note any speaking engagements, media mentions, guest posts
- Check podcast platforms for new appearances
- Pull social media metrics (use platform native analytics)
- Update tracking spreadsheet with new data

**Step 2: Grade Each Axis (5 minutes)**
Update grade for each axis using current metrics and rubric. Grade should move up, down, or stay same.
- Look for month-over-month change: +/- direction
- Assess trajectory: accelerating, stable, or declining?

**Step 3: Create MAA Summary (5 minutes)**
Format the report using the MAA model:

```
# [Client Name] Influence Report Card — [Month/Year]

## Current Grades
- Web Presence: B (was C last month) ↑ +150 organic visitors/month
- Public Speaking: C (unchanged)
- Publishing: B (was C last month) ↑ 3 new guest posts
- Podcasting: D (unchanged)
- Social Media: C (was D last month) ↑ LinkedIn +200 followers

**Overall Score: C+ (improving)**

## Key Metrics Summary
| Axis | This Month | Last Month | Change | Target |
|------|-----------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Monthly organic traffic | 650 | 500 | +30% | 2,000 |
| LinkedIn followers | 1,850 | 1,650 | +200 | 5,000 |
| Speaking engagements (YTD) | 4 | 3 | +1 | 8-12 |
| Blog posts published | 2 | 1 | +1 | 8-10/year |
| Avg social engagement | 2.1% | 1.8% | +0.3% | 5%+ |

## Analysis: What's Working
[2-3 bullets on momentum/wins]

## Analysis: Where It's Flat
[2-3 bullets on stalled areas]

## Recommended Actions (Next 30 Days)
1. [Specific, measurable action tied to weakest axis]
2. [Action to maintain momentum in strongest axis]
3. [Quick win from content factory or promotion]
4. [Speaking engagement to book or podcast to pitch]

## 6-Month Blueprint Progress
[Track client toward year-end goal using Timeline tracking table — see below]
```

### Phase 2: Creating the 6-Month Blueprint

At initial kickoff, establish a 6-month roadmap with quarterly milestones.

**6-Month Timeline Template**

```
# 6-Month Influence Blueprint — [Client Name]

## Starting Point (Baseline)
- Web Presence: C (200 organic visitors/month)
- Public Speaking: D (2 engagements/year pace)
- Publishing: C (0.5 posts/month)
- Podcasting: D (0 activity)
- Social Media: D (800 LinkedIn followers)
**Overall: D+ (foundational work needed)**

## End-State Vision (Month 6)
- Web Presence: B (1,500+ organic visitors/month)
- Public Speaking: B (8+ engagements/year pace)
- Publishing: B (2+ posts/month, 3-4 guest posts)
- Podcasting: C (4+ guest appearances)
- Social Media: B (3,000+ LinkedIn followers, 3%+ engagement)
**Overall: B (trusted authority in niche)**

## Quarterly Milestones

### Q1 (Months 1-3): Foundation
**Goal: Move from D+ to C overall**

| Axis | Month 1 Target | Month 3 Target | Actions |
|------|---|---|---|
| Web | 300 visitors/mo | 500 visitors/mo | SEO audit, fix technical issues, start blog |
| Speaking | 1 booking | 2 bookings | Pitch 10 shows, guest podcast strategy |
| Publishing | 1 post/month | 2 posts/month | Content Factory production, 1 guest post |
| Podcasting | 0 | 1 appearance | Identify 20 target podcasts |
| Social | 1K followers | 1.5K followers | Daily LinkedIn posts, engage community |

### Q2 (Months 4-6): Acceleration
**Goal: Move from C to B- overall**

| Axis | Month 4 Target | Month 6 Target | Actions |
|------|---|---|---|
| Web | 800 visitors/mo | 1,200 visitors/mo | Advanced SEO, internal linking, backlink strategy |
| Speaking | 2 bookings | 4 total YTD | Conference applications, tier-1 podcast targets |
| Publishing | 2 posts/mo | 2+ posts/mo + 2 guests | Guest post outreach, syndication |
| Podcasting | 2 appearances | 4 total | Repeat appearance strategy |
| Social | 2K followers | 3K followers | Ads on proven content, engagement strategy |

**Monthly Check-In Cadence**: 1st of each month (15 min video call with client)
```

### Common Report Card Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Strong Web, Weak Social**
- **Analysis**: Client has authority but isn't visible on social
- **Actions**:
  1. Repurpose blog content to LinkedIn weekly (use Content Factory)
  2. Share snippets, stats, frameworks from blog posts
  3. Target Dollar-a-Day promotion to blog posts to drive social traffic back to web
  4. Establish LinkedIn posting routine (3-5x/week minimum)

**Scenario 2: Speaking Active, Publishing Dormant**
- **Analysis**: Client is busy with speaking; content publishing fell off
- **Actions**:
  1. Capture audio from speaking engagements and convert to blog posts (Content Factory Produce → Process)
  2. Create podcast transcription workflow
  3. Commit to 1 guest post/month (outsource to writer if needed)
  4. Set blog publishing as non-negotiable (use scheduler)

**Scenario 3: Strong Social, Weak Web**
- **Analysis**: Client has visibility but it's not owned
- **Actions**:
  1. Drive social traffic to website with clear CTAs
  2. Improve website SEO (audit keywords, fix technical issues)
  3. Convert top social posts to blog posts
  4. Add email signup to website (capture social followers)

**Scenario 4: Stalled Across All Axes**
- **Analysis**: Missing execution, unclear priorities, or content factory not running
- **Actions**:
  1. Diagnose: What's blocking? (time, resources, clarity, motivation)
  2. Simplify: Pick ONE axis to improve (usually web or social first)
  3. Create micro-goals: Instead of "2 posts/month," aim for "1 LinkedIn post/week"
  4. Automate/outsource: Use scheduling tools, AI, ghostwriter
  5. Reset expectations: Success is improvement, not perfection

## Quality Checks

### Data Quality
- ✓ All metrics come from native platform analytics (GA4, LinkedIn, etc.), not estimates
- ✓ Tracking consistently: same dates, same metrics each month
- ✓ Data is 30 days old or newer (pull fresh data 1st of month)
- ✓ Backlinks/SEO data verified via SEMrush/Ahrefs (not competitor tools)
- ✓ Speaking/media mentions verified via Google Alerts or press databases

### Report Quality
- ✓ Grades are objective (based on rubric, not feelings)
- ✓ Analysis includes specific data points, not generic observations
- ✓ Actions are specific, measurable, and tied to underperforming axes
- ✓ Client quote/perspective included if available (voice of customer)
- ✓ Timeline progress clearly shown (on track, ahead, behind)

### Client Engagement
- ✓ Report delivered within 5 days of month-end
- ✓ 15-minute review call scheduled and held
- ✓ Client feedback captured and action items adjusted
- ✓ Client sees visible progress (even small wins acknowledged)
- ✓ Next month's priorities communicated clearly

## Common Pitfalls

### Reporting Pitfalls
**Problem**: Grades fluctuate wildly month-to-month
- **Why it happens**: Single month is too much noise; not enough baseline
- **Fix**: Look at 3-month averages. Small changes are natural. Grade based on trajectory, not single data point.

**Problem**: Actions are vague ("increase blog traffic")
- **Why it happens**: Analyst doesn't understand levers, client doesn't own next steps
- **Fix**: Make actions SMART: Specific (which channel), Measurable (target number), Assigned (who), Realistic, Time-bound (by when)

**Problem**: Report shows data but no connection to business results
- **Why it happens**: Influence metrics aren't tied to revenue/leads
- **Fix**: Add attribution: "This month's blog traffic came from X speaking engagement." Connect influence to business outcomes.

### Execution Pitfalls
**Problem**: Client sees grades but doesn't act on recommendations
- **Why it happens**: Too many recommendations; unclear priorities; client busy
- **Fix**: Limit to TOP 3 actions. Rank by impact/effort. Check in weekly on progress.

**Problem**: Same weak axis month after month (e.g., Podcasting always D)
- **Why it happens**: Not a priority; client doesn't own the tactic; unclear roadmap
- **Fix**: Decide: is this axis part of the 6-month goal or not? If yes, make it non-negotiable. If no, deprioritize.

**Problem**: Report quality degrades over time (data becomes outdated)
- **Why it happens**: No system; relies on manual tracking
- **Fix**: Automate: use GA4 dashboards, platform APIs where available, shared tracking sheet.

### Timeline Pitfalls
**Problem**: 6-month blueprint becomes irrelevant after Month 2
- **Why it happens**: No review/adjustment mechanism; external changes ignored
- **Fix**: Review quarterly. Adjust targets based on actual progress and market changes. Keep blueprint visible.

**Problem**: Client feels measured but not supported
- **Why it happens**: Report is audit, not coaching; analyst disappears between calls
- **Fix**: Use report as starting point for conversation. Offer specific help: "Let me help you pitch those 5 podcasts" or "I'll write that guest post outline."

## Human Gates
| Step | Gate Type | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Baseline data gathering (Phase 0, Step 1) | Execute | Pulling metrics from native platforms requires human access and verification |
| Grading each axis (Phase 1, Step 2) | Review | Grades must be validated against rubric, not subjective impression |
| MAA Analysis writing (Phase 1, Step 3) | Review | Connecting cause to effect requires human judgment |
| Client review call (monthly) | Execute | 15-minute video call maintains relationship and captures feedback |
| 6-month blueprint quarterly review (Phase 2) | Review | Adjusting targets based on progress and market changes |
| Action item prioritization | Review | Limiting to TOP 3 actions and ranking by impact/effort |

## Anti-Vandalism Checks
- [ ] Grades based on objective rubric thresholds (not feelings or client pressure)
- [ ] All metrics from native platform analytics (not estimates or third-party approximations)
- [ ] Month-over-month comparisons use consistent date ranges and data sources
- [ ] 6-month blueprint reviewed quarterly to prevent drift from reality
