# Dollar-a-Day Campaign

## Purpose
Dollar-a-Day is a systematic testing and scaling framework for paid social promotion. Rather than spending large budgets on untested content, Dollar-a-Day identifies organic winners with minimal spend ($1-5/day), tests them at scale ($5-20/day), and rotates evergreen winners into a permanent, profitable promotional machine. This SOP ensures consistent application of the three-phase model.

## When to Use
- After content has proven organic traction (1,000+ views, 100+ engagements)
- Monthly refresh of promotional calendar
- When launching seasonal campaigns
- To scale client's top-performing content consistently
- When email/organic reach plateaus (paid extends reach)

## Process

### Phase 0: Pre-Campaign Assessment

**Step 1: Evaluate Organic Performance (Review Analytics)**
Before paying for anything, verify content has earned the right to be promoted.

**Organic Success Metrics by Content Type:**

**Video Content**
- ✓ Minimum 1,000 organic views
- ✓ Minimum engagement: 100 reactions/comments/shares combined
- ✓ Minimum watch time: 1 minute average
- ✓ If not met: Put this asset back in production, don't pay for it yet

**Blog Post**
- ✓ Minimum 500 organic visitors (from GA4)
- ✓ Minimum engagement: 50 shares/clicks
- ✓ Average session duration: 1+ minute
- ✓ Bounce rate: Below 60%

**Social Media Post (LinkedIn, Facebook)**
- ✓ Minimum 100 organic reactions/comments/shares
- ✓ Engagement rate: 3%+ (reactions + comments / followers)
- ✓ Positive sentiment in comments (not just vanity metrics)
- ✓ If post has negative comments, don't promote

**Why This Matters:**
Organic performance predicts paid performance. Content that resonates naturally will convert at lower CPC and higher engagement. Content that bombs organically won't suddenly work when you pay.

**Step 2: Content Selection & Asset Preparation**

**Asset Quality Check**
- ✓ Video: 720p minimum, captions added, 30-120 seconds ideal
- ✓ Image: High resolution (1200px+ width), optimized, legible text
- ✓ Copy: Clear, benefit-focused, fits platform's tone
- ✓ Landing page/destination: Matches ad promise, loads fast, has clear CTA

**Identify Content Themes**
Look for patterns in organic winners:

**Example Theme Patterns:**
- "Frameworks/How-Tos": How-to posts, process videos consistently win
- "Counterintuitive Takes": Posts challenging conventional wisdom perform 2x
- "Personal Stories": Client testimonial videos outperform generic content 3x
- "Stat/Data Posts": Posts with surprising numbers get high engagement

Document these patterns—they'll guide future content production and ad selection.

**Step 3: Campaign Architecture Setup**

**Account & Campaign Structure (Facebook Ads Manager / LinkedIn Campaign Manager)**

Set up structure in ad platform BEFORE creating ads:

```
Account Structure (Facebook):
├── [Client Name] Account
│   ├── Campaign: [Q2 Content Promotion] (Conversion or Video Views objective)
│   │   ├── Adset: [Blog Phase 1 Testing] ($1-5/day budget)
│   │   │   ├── Ad: Blog Post Title v1
│   │   │   └── Ad: Blog Post Title v2
│   │   ├── Adset: [Winners Phase 2] ($5-20/day budget)
│   │   │   ├── Ad: Video Variation A
│   │   │   └── Ad: Video Variation B
│   │   └── Adset: [Evergreen Greatest Hits] ($20/day ongoing)
│   │       ├── Ad: Best Blog Post
│   │       ├── Ad: Best Video
│   │       └── Ad: Case Study
```

**Budget Allocation (Monthly)**
- Phase 1 (Testing): 20% of budget ($200 if $1,000/month total)
- Phase 2 (Scaling): 50% of budget ($500)
- Phase 3 (Evergreen): 30% of budget ($300)

### Phase 1: 7-Day Testing ($1-5/day)

**Objective**: Identify winners with minimal spend. Answer: "Does this content resonate when promoted?"

**Step 1: Ad Setup**
Choose one piece of organic-winning content (video, blog post, or social post).

**For Video Content:**
1. Use original video file or YouTube/Facebook native upload
2. Write 2-3 ad copy variations:
   - **Version A (Curiosity)**: "This one trick changed everything" [specific benefit]
   - **Version B (Direct)**: Benefit statement directly
   - **Version C (Story)**: "I learned this the hard way. Here's what happened..."
3. Create thumbnail image (if YouTube) — make it compelling (high contrast, emotion)
4. Set destination: YouTube video, Facebook video, or landing page (wherever viewers should go)

**For Blog Content:**
1. Create 2-3 image variations for ad (use featured image, or create new one)
   - **Image A**: Featured image as-is
   - **Image B**: Designed graphic with headline + key stat
   - **Image C**: Quote image with client photo
2. Write 2-3 ad copy variations (blog-specific)
   - **Version A**: Question that hooks + "See inside"
   - **Version B**: Stat or surprising insight
   - **Version C**: Benefit statement + CTA
3. Set destination: Full blog post URL (not homepage)

**For LinkedIn:**
- Copy: 1-3 ad variations (longer-form acceptable)
- Image: Featured image or carousel slides
- Destination: Article page or LinkedIn native document

**Step 2: Targeting Setup**

**Primary Audience (Start Narrow)**
1. **Location**: Client's service area (if B2B local) or target market
2. **Age**: Likely customer age range
3. **Interests**: 5-10 interests related to niche
   - Example for growth marketing: "Growth marketing," "SaaS," "Digital marketing," "Startups," "Content marketing"
4. **Behaviors** (if available): Job title, income level, etc.
5. **Custom Audience** (if available): Existing email list, website visitors

**Audience Size:** Aim for 50K-500K people (too small = limited reach, too large = wasted spend)

**Exclude:**
- Existing customers (if this is lead gen)
- People who already visited landing page (if this is repeat promotion)

**Step 3: Budget & Schedule**

**Daily Budget: $1-5/day total**
- If testing 3 ad variations: $1-2 per variation
- Focus is learning, not scale
- Run for exactly 7 days (Monday-Sunday for consistency)

**Schedule:**
- Start: Tuesday morning (avoid Monday, aim for business hours for most audiences)
- End: Monday night (exactly 7 days later)
- Run continuously (all hours — let algorithm optimize)

**Step 4: Tracking Setup**

**Metrics to Monitor (Daily)**
1. **Cost Per Click (CPC)**: How much per click to website/video
2. **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: % of impressions that clicked
3. **Cost Per View** (if video): Cost per 3-second video view
4. **Cost Per Engagement** (if social): Cost per like/comment/share
5. **Total Spend**: Verify you're on budget

**Where to Track:**
- Facebook Ads Manager: Columns > Customize columns, add CPC, CTR
- LinkedIn Campaign Manager: Campaign analytics dashboard
- Spreadsheet backup: Copy daily metrics to sheet for trend analysis

**Reporting Template (Daily):**
```
Date: [Day of Week]
Campaign: [Content Title]

Ad Variation | Impressions | Clicks | CPC | CTR | Spend
Ad Copy A   | 2,400       | 120    | $0.83 | 5% | $100
Ad Copy B   | 1,800       | 72     | $1.39 | 4% | $100
Video A     | 3,200       | 48     | $2.08 | 1.5% | $100
```

**Step 5: Decision Rules (Make Decisions Daily)**

**Pause This Ad If (Kill Rule):**
- CPC > $2.50 (uneconomical)
- CTR < 1.5% on video, < 3% on images
- Engagement rate < 2%
- Negative sentiment in comments (pause immediately, don't wait 7 days)

**Keep Running If (Keeper Rule):**
- CPC < $1.00
- CTR > 3% on images, > 2% on video
- Positive engagement, relevant comments
- Shows momentum (trending down in cost, up in engagement)

**Pause & Reflect If (Marginal Rule):**
- CPC $1.00-$1.50
- CTR 2-3%
- Outcome unclear
- **Action**: Keep running but switch copy or image. Test new variation instead of repeating marginal winner.

**Example Phase 1 Decision (Day 4 of 7):**
```
Campaign: "How to Build a Content System" Blog Post

| Variation | CPC | CTR | Decision |
|-----------|-----|-----|----------|
| Copy A (Curiosity) | $0.67 | 5.2% | ✓ KEEP — Kill copy B, test new image |
| Copy B (Direct) | $1.68 | 2.1% | ✗ PAUSE — This copy not landing |
| Copy C (Story) | $0.89 | 3.8% | ✓ KEEP — This is our second winner |
| Image A (Original) | $1.12 | 3.1% | ✓ KEEP |
| Image B (Quote) | $0.71 | 4.9% | ✓✓ WINNER — Best performer, use in Phase 2 |

Action: Pause Copy B. Test new Copy D (product angle). Scale Image B.
```

### Phase 2: 30-Day Scaling ($5-20/day per winner)

**Objective**: Take Phase 1 winners and scale to profitability. Answer: "What's the real ROI at higher spend?"

**Step 1: Consolidate Winners**
From Phase 1, identify which combinations worked:
- Best ad copy variation
- Best image/creative
- Best audience targeting
- Best CPC/CTR combo

Example: "Copy A + Image B + Audience: [Interest X]" performed best.

**Step 2: Scale Budget & Test Variations**
- **Budget**: 2-4x Phase 1 budget (if Phase 1 was $3-5/day, Phase 2 is $10-20/day)
- **Variations**: Create 3-5 ad variations to test:
  - Vary copy (different angle, emotion, CTA)
  - Vary image (if budget allows test 2-3 creatives)
  - Keep audience consistent (don't change targeting yet)

**Ad Variation Testing Example:**
```
Campaign: "Greatest Hits" (Blog + Video Bundle)

Adset 1: [Blog Post] - $10/day
├── Ad 1A: Original winning copy + Image B
├── Ad 1B: New copy variation (benefit angle) + Image B
├── Ad 1C: New copy variation (proof angle) + Image C

Adset 2: [Video] - $10/day
├── Ad 2A: Original winning video + Copy A (curiosity)
├── Ad 2B: Same video + Copy B (direct benefit)
├── Ad 2C: New video variation (testimonial) + Copy A
```

**Step 3: Audience Testing (Expansion)**
Expand targeting gradually to find lookalike/similar audiences:

**Day 1-10 (Warm Audience)**
- Keep original targeting from Phase 1
- Monitor baseline CPC and CTR
- Collect conversions data (tracking pixel)

**Day 11-20 (Warm + Lookalike)**
- Add lookalike audience (if available on platform)
- Add custom audience from email/website visitors
- Monitor if CPC increases (indicates colder audience)

**Day 21-30 (Interest Expansion)**
- Expand interest targeting to adjacent interests
- Remove underperforming interests
- Test retargeting (website visitors, video viewers)

**Step 4: Conversion Tracking**

**Set Up Conversion Goals:**
1. **Primary**: What action proves success?
   - Email signup → Count conversions in email platform
   - Lead form submission → Track via platform
   - Phone call → CallRail integration
   - Product purchase → Shopify/payment processor integration

2. **Setup in Ad Platform:**
   - Facebook Ads: Create conversion pixel for landing page
   - LinkedIn: Set up Lead Form, conversion tracking
   - Verify pixels firing (test with browser console or TagAssistant)

**Metrics to Track (Phase 2):**
| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Cost Per Click (CPC) | <$1.50 | >$2.00 |
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | >2% | <1% |
| Cost Per Conversion (CPA) | <30% of CLV* | >50% of CLV |
| Conversion Rate | >2% | <0.5% |
| Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) | >2:1 | <1:1 |

*CLV = Customer Lifetime Value (estimate what a customer is worth to business)

**Step 5: Daily Optimization (Phase 2)**

**Workflow:**
- **Daily** (first 10 days): Monitor CPC, pause underperformers
- **Every 3 days**: Review conversion data, reallocate budget to winners
- **Bi-weekly** (Day 15, Day 30): Full performance review, pause/scale decisions

**Daily Decision Rules:**

**Pause If:**
- CPA > 50% of customer lifetime value
- CTR declining 3+ days in a row (ad fatigue)
- Negative comments or brand safety issue
- CPC > 2x original Phase 1 winning CPC

**Double Budget If:**
- CPA < 30% of customer lifetime value
- ROAS > 2:1 (making 2x return)
- CTR stable or increasing
- Positive customer feedback

**Test New Creative If:**
- CPC creeping up (sign of fatigue)
- CTR declining but CPA still good
- Want to refresh messaging for next phase

**Example Phase 2 Optimization (Week 2):**
```
Campaign: "Blog Post — Greatest Hits"

Current Performance:
- Ad 1A (Original): CPA $15, ROAS 3:1, 50 conversions ✓✓ SCALE TO $20/day
- Ad 1B (New copy): CPA $28, ROAS 1.2:1, 15 conversions → Keep testing, may need new image
- Ad 1C (Proof angle): CPA $42, ROAS 0.8:1, 10 conversions ✗ PAUSE

Action:
- Increase Ad 1A budget from $10 to $20/day
- Pause Ad 1C, test new image variation with Copy 1B
- Move some budget from Ad 1B to Ad 1A
- New spend allocation: $20 (1A) + $5 (1B test) = $25 total
```

**Step 6: End-of-Phase-2 Decision**

**Decide: Evergreen or Kill?**

At Day 30, evaluate overall performance:

**Promote to Phase 3 (Evergreen) If:**
- CPA < 40% of CLV
- Stable conversions (not declining)
- Positive sentiment
- High quality leads/customers (not just volume)
- Scalable (budget can go 2-3x without hurting performance)

**Kill Campaign If:**
- CPA > 50% of CLV
- Declining conversions over final 10 days
- Negative feedback
- Low quality leads
- Market saturation (everyone's seen the ad)

**Iterate (Try New Angle) If:**
- CPA marginally profitable ($40-50 of CLV)
- Declining but not dead
- **Action**: Go back to Phase 1 with new creative/copy angle

### Phase 3: Evergreen "Greatest Hits" Rotation ($20-50/day ongoing)

**Objective**: Maintain always-on lead generation machine with rotation to prevent fatigue.

**Step 1: Build "Greatest Hits" Collection**
Consolidate all Phase 2 winners into one pool:

**Example Collection (5-10 assets):**
1. Blog Post A (How-to format) — $0.65 CPC
2. Video B (Testimonial) — $0.82 CPC
3. Blog Post C (Controversy) — $0.71 CPC
4. Case Study D — $0.58 CPC (BEST PERFORMER)
5. Video E (Q&A) — $0.94 CPC
6. Blog Post F (Resource guide) — $0.73 CPC
[+4-5 more winners]

**Step 2: Campaign Setup (Evergreen)**

**Budget Allocation:**
- Total: $20-50/day or $600-1,500/month
- Divide by # assets: 10 assets × $2/day each = $20 total
- Rotate: Run each asset in round-robin (fair share)

**Alternative: Audience-Based Rotation**
- Adset 1 (Warm audience): $10/day — top 3 best performers
- Adset 2 (Custom audience): $10/day — mid-tier performers
- Adset 3 (Lookalike): $15/day — all 5-10 performers

**Step 3: Ad Fatigue Management**

**Watch For Ad Fatigue Signs:**
- CTR declining week-over-week
- CPC increasing steadily
- Impressions growing but clicks flat
- Conversion rate deteriorating

**Fatigue Refresh Cycle (Monthly):**
1. Review performance of all 5-10 assets
2. Keep top 5 (best CPA/ROAS)
3. Retire bottom 2-3 (freshest audience perspective)
4. Add 1-2 new creative variations
5. Pause and rotate: Rest underperformers for 2-3 weeks, bring back later

**Example Monthly Rotation:**
```
Week 1: Run Top 5 performers + 1 new test
Week 2: Rotate out worst performer, keep Top 5 + new test
Week 3: Rotate out 2nd worst, bring back a previously rested asset
Week 4: Full review, retire bottom 3, add 2 new tests

This keeps audience fresh while maintaining proven winners.
```

**Step 4: Monthly Reporting & Optimization**

**Monthly Review (1st of month, 1 hour):**

**Metrics Dashboard:**
```

| Asset | CPC | CPA | Conv | ROAS | Status |
|-------|-----|-----|------|------|--------|

## Underperformers (At Risk)
| Asset | CPC | CPA | Conv | ROAS | Status |
|-------|-----|-----|------|------|--------|
| Video #5 | $0.94 | $45 | 20 | 1.4:1 | ⚠ Rotate |
| Blog #3 | $1.10 | $52 | 15 | 1.1:1 | ✗ Pause |

## New Tests (Week in)
| Asset | CPC | CTR | Conv | Status |
|-------|-----|-----|------|--------|
| Blog #7 | $0.88 | 2.1% | 12 | Testing |

## Summary
- Total Spend: $1,245 (on budget: $1,500)
- Total Conversions: 290
- Avg CPA: $4.29
- Avg ROAS: 2.9:1
- Ad Fatigue: Slight (CTR down 0.3% MoM)

## Actions Next Month
1. Scale Blog #4 to $5/day (from $2)
2. Pause Video #5 (CPA too high)
```

**Decision Matrix:**
- **CPA < $20 (target CLV $75+)**: Increase budget
- **CPA $20-40**: Hold steady, monitor
- **CPA $40-50**: Test new creative, may retire
- **CPA > $50**: Pause immediately

**Step 5: Continuous Audience Refresh**

**Quarterly (Every 3 months):**
1. Assess audience fatigue across all segments
2. Add new audience segments:
   - New geographic markets
   - New interest expansions
   - New lookalike audiences
3. Retire saturated audiences (where CTR collapsed)
4. Test new demographics if customer base expanded

**Example Quarterly Expansion:**
- Started: City 1 + Industry Interest A → CPC stable
- Month 3: Add City 2 + Industry Interest B → Test at 20% budget
- If performing: Scale City 2 + Interest B
- If fatigued: Pause City 1, double down on City 2

- ✓ Optimization done every 3 days
- ✓ End-of-phase decision documented (Evergreen? Kill? Iterate?)

### Phase 3 Checkpoint
- ✓ All assets are Phase 2 winners (CPA < 50% CLV)
- ✓ Rotation schedule created (5-10 assets)
- ✓ Monthly performance review completed

- ✓ 1-2 new tests added each month

- ✓ Spend on budget (+/- 10%)

- ✓ Conversions meeting target

- ✓ No brand safety issues
- ✓ ROI sustainable (ROAS > 1.5:1 minimum)

## Common Pitfalls

### Phase 1 Pitfalls
**Problem**: Promoting content that hasn't earned organic traction
- **Why it happens**: Pressure to spend budget; over-confidence in content
- **Fix**: Enforce minimum thresholds (1,000 views, 100 engagements) before Phase 1

**Problem**: Testing too many variations simultaneously (unclear results)
- **Why it happens**: Impatience; trying to optimize too fast
- **Fix**: Limit to 2-3 variations per asset. Run 7 days minimum for data clarity.

**Problem**: Not pausing underperformers quickly (wasting budget)
- **Why it happens**: Hope they'll improve; unclear kill thresholds
- **Fix**: Create hard rules upfront (CPC > $2.50 = pause). Execute ruthlessly.

**Problem**: Changing targeting mid-test (conflates learning)
- **Why it happens**: Trying to optimize; unclear process
- **Fix**: Keep Phase 1 targeting locked. Expansion testing happens in Phase 2.

### Phase 2 Pitfalls
**Problem**: Budget 2-3x too high; runs out before learning (or blows through CLV)
- **Why it happens**: Over-confident in Phase 1 results; doesn't account for audience saturation
- **Fix**: Conservative scaling: 2x Phase 1, max. If CPA doubles, kill immediately.

**Problem**: No conversion tracking (can't calculate CPA)
- **Why it happens**: Forgot to install pixel; conversion event not set up
- **Fix**: Set up tracking Day 1 of campaign, test it. Verify conversions daily.

**Problem**: Testing new audience without baseline (can't isolate winner)
- **Why it happens**: Trying to optimize everything simultaneously
- **Fix**: Week 1 (warm audience, original targeting). Week 2-3 (expand). Day 30 (audit).

**Problem**: Killing campaigns too early (winning ads got paused before proving out)
- **Why it happens**: Impatience; low conversion volume
- **Fix**: 30-day minimum per Phase 2. Need 100+ conversions for statistical significance.

### Phase 3 Pitfalls
**Problem**: Ad fatigue not managed; CTR crashes, CPC spikes
- **Why it happens**: Running same 5 assets for 6+ months without rotation
- **Fix**: Rotate bottom 2-3 assets monthly. Add new tests. Rest tired creatives.

**Problem**: Evergreen campaign doesn't actually scale (budget stalls at $15/day)
- **Why it happens**: Audience saturated; didn't expand to new segments
- **Fix**: Quarterly audience refresh. Test new geographies, interests, lookalikes.

**Problem**: Budget creeps up but ROAS creeps down (profitability deteriorates)
- **Why it happens**: Audience fatigue + budget pressure
- **Fix**: Monitor ROAS monthly. If ROAS < 1.5:1, reduce budget. Quality > quantity.

**Problem**: Campaign runs "forever" with declining results
- **Why it happens**: No review cadence; set-it-and-forget-it mindset
- **Fix**: Monthly review mandatory. Kill underperformers quarterly.

### Tracking & Attribution Pitfalls
**Problem**: Can't attribute conversions to campaigns (no tracking)
- **Why it happens**: Pixel not installed, or wrong landing page tracked
- **Fix**: Test pixel on Day 1. Verify conversions manually (check email signup, form submission) before scaling.

**Problem**: CPA inflated because tracking only captures some conversions
- **Why it happens**: Offline conversions not tracked (phone calls, meetings)
- **Fix**: For B2B: use CallRail. For B2C: use email/form as primary conversion. Adjust CPA calculation for known offline conversions.

**Problem**: Comparing CPA across different channels (apples-to-oranges)
- **Why it happens**: Facebook CPA vs LinkedIn CPA vs email — different audiences
- **Fix**: Track separately. Accept channel differences. Focus on total profit, not CPA.

## Critical Success Formula

**Dollar-a-Day works because of:**
1. **Testing discipline**: Small budgets force rigor (can't waste)
2. **Threshold enforcement**: Clear kill/scale rules prevent emotional decisions
3. **Data-driven scaling**: Only scale what proves profitable
4. **Continuous refresh**: Rotate to prevent fatigue
5. **Long-term view**: Phase 3 is designed for sustained profitability, not quick wins

**Three ways it fails:**
1. **Impatience**: Scaling before Phase 1 learning is clear
2. **Unclear thresholds**: No kill rules; hope-based decisions
3. **Set-and-forget**: No monthly review or rotation

**Success metrics:**
- CPA < 40% of customer lifetime value
- Scaling Phase 2 winners 2-3x over Phase 1 without losing efficiency
- Evergreen campaign maintains 2+ ROAS with monthly refresh
