# Content Factory Execution

## Purpose
The Content Factory is a systematized 6-stage process to capture, process, distribute, and amplify content across all client channels. Raw material comes from the client's existing activities (speaking, meetings, calls) — the owner produces 1%, the system handles the other 99%.

This SOP follows the canonical BlitzMetrics 6-stage model: **Plumbing → Produce → Process → Post → Promote → Perform**. After initial setup, stages 2-6 run as a continuous loop, with Perform feeding insights back into Process.

**Anti-vandalism:** Do not reduce the 6 stages to fewer. Do not remove MAA from any stage. Do not remove the Content Library requirement. Do not remove human-required designations. Any structural change must reference the canon source and update this compliance table.

## When to Use
- At account kickoff for new clients (start at Plumbing)
- Monthly content planning cycles (Produce → Perform loop)
- When client has major speaking events, podcasts, or recorded activities
- To scale proven content pieces through Dollar-a-Day amplification

## Prerequisites
- GCT (Goals, Content, Targeting) form completed for the client
- Client has agreed to content capture (video/audio recording permissions)
- Access Checklist completed (all platform logins granted and documented)
- Content Library spreadsheet created for this client

## Stage 0: Plumbing (Foundation Setup)

**Purpose:** Set up the foundational digital infrastructure. Done once at onboarding, maintained ongoing.

**Triangle context:** GCT must be completed before Plumbing begins. Plumbing enables all downstream stages.

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] GCT form completed with client
- [ ] Client has provided business information (hours, services, locations)
- [ ] Access Checklist initiated — client knows what logins to provide
- [ ] Budget and timeline agreed

### Execution Checklist

**Google Business Profile**
1. Claim and verify GBP listing
2. Complete all profile fields: business name, address, phone, hours, categories
3. Add high-quality geotagged photos (minimum 10): storefront, team, office, products/services
4. Write business description (2-3 sentences with keywords)
5. Set up GBP Posts scheduling

**Tracking & Analytics**
1. Create Google Analytics 4 property linked to website
2. Verify Google Search Console
3. Install remarketing pixels: Facebook, Google, cross-channel
4. Set up UTM parameter system:
   - Format: `?utm_source=[channel]&utm_medium=[type]&utm_campaign=[name]`
5. Configure goal/conversion tracking in GA4
6. Set up call tracking (CallRail or similar) if B2B service business

**Website & Social Profiles**
1. Verify website loads fast, has proper structure, schema.org markup
2. Ensure consistent branding across all social profiles
3. Set up CRM integration if applicable

**Workflow Setup**
1. Create Content Library (Google Sheet) — the central tracking tool for all assets
2. Set up content calendar (Asana, Monday.com, or Notion)
3. Create approval workflow: Draft → Client Review → Publish
4. Set up scheduling tools (Buffer/Later for social, native platform schedulers)

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] All items on Digital Plumbing Checklist completed
- [ ] All access granted and documented via Access Checklist
- [ ] GA4 firing correctly on all pages
- [ ] Remarketing audiences building
- [ ] Content Library created and shared with team
- [ ] Content calendar created with first month planned

### MAA for Plumbing
- **Metrics:** Number of plumbing items completed vs. total. Any access still pending.
- **Analysis:** What's blocking completion? Which items are client-dependent?
- **Action:** Specific follow-ups with deadlines to close remaining gaps.

### Human Required
- Client must provide business information and platform access
- Team lead reviews and verifies all tracking is firing correctly

## Stage 1: Produce (Capture Raw Material)

**Purpose:** Generate raw content that proves you do what you say you do, in the places you say you do it in. This is the 1% the business owner must personally contribute.

**Triangle context:** ACC — capture content for all three funnel stages (WHY/HOW/WHAT). The owner's authentic stories are irreplaceable.

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Plumbing stage complete (tracking in place to measure what we produce)
- [ ] Topic Wheel created for client (content themes organized by expertise area)
- [ ] GCT defines what content is needed per ACC stage
- [ ] Recording permissions confirmed

### Execution Checklist

**Content Capture Triggers**
- Record all client Zoom calls (with permission) — unfiltered, authentic material
- Audio from speaking engagements, conferences, webinars
- Podcast appearances (guest or host)
- Geotagged photos from events, office, team activities
- One-minute videos: WHY video (mission/purpose), PAA answers, client stories
- Three-minute interviews with speakers/authorities
- Existing content: LinkedIn posts, email newsletters, testimonials

**The 4 Components of a One-Minute Video**
1. Hook (0-3 seconds) — capture attention immediately
2. Ignite pain/pleasure (3-15 seconds) — present problem or opportunity
3. Describe solution (15-50 seconds) — show what you offer
4. Call to Action (50-60 seconds) — tell them what to do

**Capture Quality Standards**
- Video: minimum 720p, ideally 1080p
- Audio: 44.1kHz or higher
- Photos: high resolution (3000px+ width), geotagged when possible
- Transcribe all video/audio within 48 hours of capture
- Content must be AUTHENTIC — real photos, real videos, real locations

**Capture Organization**
1. Create shared folder structure:
   ```
   /Client Name/Content Capture/
   ├── Raw Video
   ├── Raw Audio
   ├── Photos
   ├── Transcripts
   └── Metadata (dates, speakers, topics)
   ```
2. Tag all files with date, source, and key topics
3. Log every captured asset in the Content Library immediately

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] All captured assets logged in Content Library
- [ ] Transcripts completed within 48 hours
- [ ] Content tagged by ACC stage (Awareness/Consideration/Conversion)
- [ ] Content mapped to Topic Wheel categories
- [ ] Quality standards met (resolution, audio, geotagging)

### MAA for Produce
- **Metrics:** Number of raw assets captured this week. Hours of video/audio. Photos by location. Breakdown by ACC stage.
- **Analysis:** Are we capturing enough content across all ACC stages? Is the owner participating in the 1% they need to? Any gaps in Topic Wheel coverage?
- **Action:** Schedule specific capture sessions. Request specific content types from client. Target weakest ACC stage.

### Human Required
- Business owner MUST produce raw content. No one else can tell their stories.
- Owner records WHY video and answers PAA questions personally.
- Client approves which recordings can be used for content.

## Stage 2: Process (Transform into Assets)

**Purpose:** Transform raw content into multiple publishable assets using AI tools and VAs. This is the multiplication stage — one recording becomes 10+ assets.

**Triangle context:** CCS — AI assists but does not replace. Content must be sprinkled with real stories and photos for E-E-A-T. ChatGPT is an assistant, not a replacement for your VA.

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Raw content available in shared folder
- [ ] Transcripts completed
- [ ] Topic Wheel available for content categorization
- [ ] Content Library ready to receive processed assets
- [ ] Design Style Guide available for visual processing

### Execution Checklist

**Blog Post Creation**
1. Take transcript from client recording
2. Use AI (Claude/ChatGPT) to:
   - Create headline with primary keyword
   - Structure: intro (hook + context) → body (3-5 key points) → conclusion
   - Optimize for E-E-A-T: weave in client's real experience and expertise
   - Add internal links to relevant pages
   - Target 1,200-2,000 words for SEO value
3. Include: author bio with credibility markers, featured image, meta description
4. Add CTA related to specific offer/next step
5. Organize by Topic Wheel category

**Short-Form Video Clips**
1. Identify 30-90 second segments from longer video that:
   - Answer a specific question (PAA)
   - Make a bold/memorable statement
   - Show personality/unique perspective
2. Use Descript or CapCut to:
   - Add captions (essential for all social platforms)
   - Include logo/branding
   - Add B-roll if available
   - Optimize for platform dimensions (9:16 vertical, 1:1 square, 16:9 landscape)
3. Export at platform-native quality

**Social Media Posts**
1. Extract 5-10 key quotes/insights from longer content
2. Expand into platform-specific posts:
   - **LinkedIn**: Professional tone, 100-150 words, hashtags, CTA
   - **Facebook**: Conversational, 50-100 words, calls engagement
   - **Instagram**: Visual-first, caption 75-150 words, 20-30 hashtags
   - **TikTok**: Personality-driven, text overlay, native audio
3. Create 3-5 caption variations for video posts

**Email Newsletter Segments**
1. Extract 2-3 best insights from content
2. Create: subject line with curiosity gap, preview text, hero image, 100-150 word summary, link to full content

**Visual Assets (Infographics)**
1. Identify frameworks, how-tos, or comparisons from client content
2. Create in Canva/Figma: process flowcharts, comparison tables, stat callout graphics
3. Include branding, white space, readable fonts (14pt minimum)

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] All processed assets tracked in Content Library with status
- [ ] E-E-A-T: real stories and photos woven into every piece
- [ ] Tone matches client's voice (not over-processed or generic)
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words of articles
- [ ] Fact-checking complete: claims accurate or marked as opinion
- [ ] CTA is clear and specific
- [ ] Visual assets are on-brand and high quality
- [ ] No orphaned links or broken references

### MAA for Process
- **Metrics:** Assets processed this week by type (articles, videos, social posts, visuals). Processing time per asset. Content Library growth.
- **Analysis:** Is the processing pipeline keeping up with raw content capture? What asset types are we overproducing or underproducing relative to channel needs? Quality holding steady?
- **Action:** Adjust processing priorities based on channel performance data from Perform stage. Target asset types with best ROI.

### Human Required
- QA review of all AI-processed content before it moves to Post
- Voice/tone approval — client signs off on key pieces
- E-E-A-T compliance check: does this contain real experience, not just AI generation?

## Stage 3: Post (Publish Across Channels)

**Purpose:** Distribute processed content across all channels with consistent timing and tracking.

**Triangle context:** ACC — distribute content that matches each platform's audience stage. All content cross-posted to Content Library with URLs.

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Content has passed Process stage verification
- [ ] Client approval received (if required for this content type)
- [ ] Content Library entry exists for every asset being posted
- [ ] Scheduling tools configured and tested

### Execution Checklist

**Content Calendar & Scheduling**
1. Plan 30 days of content in advance using Topic Wheel categories
2. Distribution cadence:
   - Blog posts: 1-2 per week (Tuesday-Thursday optimal for B2B)
   - Social media: Daily on LinkedIn, 3-5x weekly on Facebook/Instagram
   - Email: 1-2x per week (Tuesday-Thursday optimal)
   - YouTube: 1 video per week if volume allows
   - Google Business Profile: Weekly (posts expire after 7 days)

**Channel-Specific Publishing**

*Blog/Website*
1. Create post in WordPress/CMS organized by Topic Wheel category
2. Set featured image and meta description
3. Add internal links to 2-3 existing pages
4. Set SEO settings (RankMath or Yoast)
5. Add schema.org markup
6. Preview on mobile
7. Publish and verify on live site

*YouTube/Video*
1. Upload in highest quality format
2. Title: 60 chars max, includes keyword
3. Description: hook → takeaways → links → timestamps
4. Tags: primary + 5 secondary keywords
5. Custom thumbnail (1280x720px, high contrast)
6. Add to relevant playlist

*LinkedIn*
1. Post to client's personal profile first (priority reach), then company page
2. Longer-form posts (up to 3,000 chars), line breaks for readability
3. 5-8 relevant hashtags
4. Schedule for morning business hours (7-9am local client time)
5. Target: 10 engagements in first hour for algorithm boost

*Facebook & Instagram*
1. Format images for platform dimensions
2. Write caption per platform guidelines
3. Tag relevant people/pages
4. Schedule or post immediately
5. Engage: respond to comments within first hour

*Google Business Profile*
1. Post with image/video, headline (3-4 words), description (300 chars max)
2. Include CTA (Learn More, Call, Book)
3. Rotate fresh content weekly (posts expire after 7 days)

*Email*
1. Compelling subject line (50 chars, benefit-driven)
2. Set preview text explicitly
3. Mobile preview verified
4. Schedule for optimal send time (Tue-Thu 9am)

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] Content live on all intended channels
- [ ] All posted URLs recorded in Content Library
- [ ] Links working (retest 24 hours later)
- [ ] Formatting correct on mobile, tablet, desktop
- [ ] Analytics tracking firing (GA4, UTM parameters)
- [ ] Consistent messaging across channels (adapt format, not message)

### MAA for Post
- **Metrics:** Posts published this week by channel. Publishing cadence adherence (planned vs. actual). Content Library entries with URLs.
- **Analysis:** Are we hitting our publishing cadence? Which channels are falling behind? Any bottlenecks in the approval workflow?
- **Action:** Adjust scheduling, fix bottlenecks, reallocate effort to underserved channels.

### Human Required
- WordPress publishing (login, featured image, RankMath/SEO settings)
- Client approval before publishing to their personal channels
- First-hour engagement on social posts

## Stage 4: Promote (Amplify with Dollar-a-Day)

**Purpose:** Amplify best content using Dollar-a-Day and Thank You Machine. Only boost content that has proven organic engagement first.

**Triangle context:** ACC — promote content at every funnel stage. GCT targeting drives audience selection. Never skip organic proof.

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Content has been posted (Stage 3) and collected organic engagement data
- [ ] Organic thresholds met before spending money:
  - Video: 1,000+ organic views or 100+ engagements
  - Blog post: 500+ organic visitors or 50+ shares
  - Social post: 100+ likes/reactions or 20+ comments
  - Email: 25%+ open rate
- [ ] Ad account set up and pixels verified
- [ ] Budget approved by client

### Execution Checklist

**Dollar-a-Day Phases**

*Phase 1: 7-Day Testing ($1-5/day per asset)*
1. Select 1-3 proven organic winners
2. Create ad from existing creative (1:1 or 16:9)
3. Set targeting using GCT:
   - Location: client's service area
   - Interests: from Topic Wheel and GCT research
   - Custom audience: email list, website visitors
4. Run 7 days to collect data
5. Decision rule:
   - **Kill**: CPC > $2 or engagement rate < 2%
   - **Advance**: CPC < $1 or engagement rate > 5%

*Phase 2: 30-Day Scaling ($5-20/day per winner)*
1. Scale budget on Phase 1 winners
2. Add 2-3 ad variations (different copy, image, CTA)
3. Expand targeting: lookalike audiences, broader interests, wider geography
4. Monitor daily: pause underperformers after 3 days
5. Scale aggressively if CPA < 1/3 of customer lifetime value

*Phase 3: Evergreen "Greatest Hits" Rotation ($20-50/day)*
1. Consolidate 5-10 best-performing assets into rotation
2. Always-on campaign: consistent daily budget, top variations in round-robin
3. Refresh creative monthly (swap in new tested assets)
4. Monitor for ad fatigue (declining CTR, increasing CPC)
5. Maintain indefinitely as lead generation machine

**Thank You Machine**
1. Track positive mentions (mention.com or native monitoring)
2. Ask permission to quote: "Thank you! Can I quote you on that?"
3. Track in Positive Mention Bank spreadsheet
4. Send personalized gifts (custom stamps, postcards, Amazon gifts)
5. Create content that elevates others (roundups, listicles, visual media)
6. Amplify client love content via Dollar-a-Day

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] Only organically proven content is being promoted (no skipping Phase 1)
- [ ] Every promoted piece tracked in Content Library with spend data
- [ ] Ad creative matches landing page/destination
- [ ] Copy is honest and specific (not clickbait)
- [ ] Targeting is relevant (no wasted impressions)
- [ ] Frequency cap set (12-15 impressions/person/week max)
- [ ] Budget tracked by asset and channel

### MAA for Promote
- **Metrics:** Ad spend by phase and asset. CPC, CTR, CPA, ROAS. Engagement rate by ad. Thank You Machine mentions collected.
- **Analysis:** Which assets are earning their spend? Which phases should assets advance to or be killed? Is targeting efficient or too broad?
- **Action:** Kill underperformers. Advance winners. Adjust targeting. Refresh fatigued creative. Set next week's budget allocation.

### Human Required
- Budget approval for scaling from Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Phase 3
- Gift personalization for Thank You Machine
- Relationship management with clients and partners mentioned

## Stage 5: Perform (Measure, Analyze, Act)

**Purpose:** Apply MAA rigorously to the entire Content Factory cycle. Identify winners, kill losers, and feed insights back into Process. This stage drives the continuous loop.

**Triangle context:** MAA is the engine of this stage. Performance data flows back into all other stages. Content scored on the 30-point authority scale (What x Who x Where).

### Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Content has been live long enough to collect meaningful data (minimum 7 days for organic, 3 days for paid)
- [ ] Analytics access confirmed (GA4, platform native analytics, ad dashboards)
- [ ] Content Library up to date with all posted URLs and promotion data

### Execution Checklist

**Weekly MAA Cycle (Every Friday)**
1. **Metrics:** Pull actual numbers from native platforms:
   - Website: sessions, bounce rate, conversions, top pages
   - Social: reach, engagement rate, followers gained, top posts
   - Email: open rate, click rate, unsubscribes
   - Paid: spend, CPC, CTR, CPA, ROAS by asset
   - Video: views, watch time, completion rate
   - Week-over-week comparisons for all key metrics
2. **Analysis:** WHY are the numbers what they are?
   - What content type is performing best and why?
   - Which ACC stage is weakest?
   - What's the connection between capture quality and performance?
   - Are we building authority (30-point scale: What x Who x Where)?
   - Call out the single weakest area explicitly
   - This is NOT a task list — it's insight
3. **Action:** Specific next steps with owner and deadline:
   - At least one action targets the weakest metric
   - Minimum 3 action items
   - Feed winners back into Process for repurposing
   - Kill or rework losers
   - Each action has a named owner and due date

**Identify Hot Content (4 Signals)**
- High organic engagement relative to baseline
- Comments asking questions or requesting more
- Shares/saves (strongest signal of value)
- External mentions or links

**Content Authority Scoring**
- Score content on 30-point scale: What (content quality, 1-10) x Who (creator authority, 1-10) x Where (platform authority, 1-10)
- Greatest Hits: assets scoring 20+ go into evergreen promotion
- Update Content Library with scores

**Monthly Reporting**
- Total content pieces published by type and channel
- Engagement trends by channel (month-over-month)
- Top 3 performing pieces by channel
- Paid promotion ROI by phase
- Topic Wheel coverage gaps
- Client check-in: what's resonating, what to double down on
- Plan next month's topics using Topic Wheel

**The Continuous Loop**
- Winners from Perform feed back into Process for repurposing
- Losers are analyzed to prevent repeating mistakes
- Insights from Perform adjust Produce priorities (what to capture next)
- The cycle compounds — each iteration makes the factory smarter

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] MAA report completed and posted by Friday EOD
- [ ] Content Library updated with performance data and scores
- [ ] Winners identified and queued for repurposing in Process stage
- [ ] Underperformers killed or flagged for rework
- [ ] Next week's priorities set based on data (not guesswork)
- [ ] Monthly report delivered to client (if applicable)

### MAA for Perform (Meta-MAA)
- **Metrics:** MAA report completion rate (did we do it every week?). Time from data pull to action. Number of actions completed from prior week.
- **Analysis:** Is our MAA process itself improving? Are we acting on insights or just reporting? Are actions leading to measurable improvement?
- **Action:** Improve the reporting process. Close accountability gaps. Escalate systemic issues.

### Human Required
- Analysis requires human judgment — AI can pull metrics, humans interpret them
- Strategic pivots are human decisions
- Client communication about performance and direction
- Dennis/team lead reviews and pushes back on poor-quality MAA reports

## LDT Context (Learn, Do, Teach)

The Content Factory process follows the LDT triangle for team development:

**Learn:** New team members study this SOP and the canon sources. They observe experienced team members executing each stage. They complete the BlitzMetrics MICRO courses relevant to their role.

**Do:** Team members execute each stage under supervision. Minimum 3 successful executions of a stage before they are considered competent. Proof through direct execution — not just understanding.

**Teach:** Competent team members document improvements to this SOP, train new team members, and create content demonstrating their expertise. The best team members share knowledge freely.

**Progression path:**
1. Start by learning Process stage (AI-assisted content transformation)
2. Move to Post stage (channel-specific publishing)
3. Learn Promote stage (Dollar-a-Day execution)
4. Graduate to Perform stage (MAA reporting and analysis)
5. Eventually own Plumbing and Produce coordination for new clients

## Common Pitfalls

### Production Pitfalls
**Content loses client's voice**
- Cause: AI tools used without client review; too much editing
- Fix: Client approves key pieces before full processing; maintain their unique phrases

**Content sits in draft and never publishes**
- Cause: Perfectionism; unclear approval workflow
- Fix: 80/20 rule — publish good work now, not perfect work never. Hard deadlines in content calendar.

**No consistency in posting schedule**
- Cause: Reactive publishing; no content calendar
- Fix: Plan 30 days ahead. Schedule everything in advance.

### Distribution Pitfalls
**Content published but not promoted**
- Cause: Budget not allocated; unclear responsibility
- Fix: Build promotion into process from start. Allocate 30-50% of effort to promotion.

**Promoting content that hasn't proven itself organically**
- Cause: Pressure to spend budget; skipping organic phase
- Fix: Enforce organic thresholds before paid. Phase 1 testing is mandatory.

**Different channels get different messaging**
- Cause: No content guidelines; siloed channel management
- Fix: One content asset, distributed across channels. Adapt format, not message.

### Tracking Pitfalls
**Can't attribute results to specific content**
- Cause: No UTM parameters; mixed channels in analytics
- Fix: UTM on everything. Separate tracking URLs by campaign. Review weekly.

**Volume increases but engagement decreases**
- Cause: Quantity over quality; audience fatigue
- Fix: Monitor engagement rate, not just volume. Pause underperformers. Fewer, better pieces.

**MAA reports become task lists instead of analysis**
- Cause: Team doesn't understand the difference between Analysis and Action
- Fix: Analysis answers WHY. Action answers WHAT NEXT. Review MAA reports and push back on task-list-style analysis.
