# Event Planning SOP

## Purpose
Run professional in-person events in [Your Speaker Event] format — half-day, TED Talk-style, with full content capture — and systematically turn every event into months of content output.

## When to Use
- Planning a new Forge event or similar in-person gathering
- [Your Mentor/Advisor] requests a recurring event series
- An opportunity arises for a multi-speaker, content-rich event
- Preparing post-event content processing pipeline

## Process

1. **Define the event.** Lock down: date, city, venue, format (half-day TED Talk-style), target audience, number of speakers (9 standard), and talk duration (20 minutes each). Document everything in a Basecamp project.

2. **Secure the venue.** Requirements: capacity for audience + crew, AV equipment (projector, screen, mics), access to or proximity to a podcast studio, green screen studio if possible. Confirm venue availability and book with deposit.

3. **Send speaker invitations.** Email each speaker with:
   - What [Your Speaker Event] is (half-day event, 9 speakers, TED Talk format)
   - What they'll present (their topic, 20-minute slot)
   - Logistics (date, time, location, travel details)
   - What they get (professional recording, content package, exposure)
   - What you need from them (bio, headshot, talk title, slides if applicable)

4. **Confirm all speakers by deadline.** Set the confirmation deadline at **2 weeks before promotion starts**. No confirmed speaker = find a replacement immediately. Maintain a backup speaker list.

5. **Book travel.** Use points when possible. Budget airlines (Frontier, Spirit) for cost-sensitive bookings. Book flights and hotels at least 3 weeks out. Track all travel expenses for the P&L.

6. **Plan content capture.** Gear checklist:
   - Multiple cameras (minimum 2 angles per speaker)
   - Lapel mics or podium mic with backup
   - Podcast studio access for interview sessions during breaks
   - Green screen studio for speaker intros/outros
   - B-roll equipment (handheld camera, gimbal)

7. **Execute event-day logistics.**
   - Arrive 30 minutes before first speaker
   - Print the schedule and post it visibly
   - Name badges for all attendees and speakers
   - Full AV check before doors open (test mics, cameras, projector, recording)
   - Assign someone to manage time — **strict 20-minute limit per speaker**

8. **Run the event format.**
   - 20 minutes per speaker, no exceptions
   - Networking breaks between speaker blocks
   - Podcast interviews with speakers during breaks (pre-schedule these)
   - Group dinner after the event for relationship building

9. **Process post-event content.** Turn raw footage into:
   - Full YouTube talks (one per speaker)
   - Book chapters (from talk transcripts)
   - Podcast episodes (from break interviews)
   - Speaker highlight reels (90-second cuts)
   - Short-form clips for Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, LinkedIn

10. **Boost content via Dollar-a-Day.** Run $1/day campaigns on the best-performing clips. Tag speakers. Cross-promote across all channels. See `dollar-a-day-campaign.md` for the full process.

11. **Document learnings for next event.** Within one week of the event, write a debrief: what worked, what didn't, what to change. Update this SOP with findings. [Your Mentor/Advisor] wants this recurring — every event should be better than the last.

## Quality Checks
- [ ] All speakers confirmed with bios, headshots, and talk titles
- [ ] Travel booked for all out-of-town participants
- [ ] AV equipment tested day-of before doors open
- [ ] Content capture equipment ready with backups (batteries, cards, backup recorder)
- [ ] Post-event content processing timeline set with deadlines and owners

## Common Pitfalls
- **Not enough cameras or angles.** One camera = one angle = boring content. Plan for minimum 2 angles. Three is better.
- **Poor audio in the venue.** Test mics in the actual room. Venue acoustics vary wildly. Bring a backup audio recorder (Zoom H6 or similar).
- **Speakers going over time.** 20 minutes means 20 minutes. Assign a timekeeper. Use a visible countdown timer. Brief speakers beforehand — going over steals time from the next person.
- **No post-event content plan.** The event is the raw material, not the deliverable. If you don't have a processing plan before the event, the footage sits unused. Assign owners and deadlines before event day.
- **Not promoting the output.** Recording content and not distributing it is wasted effort. Every talk should become at least 5 pieces of content, boosted via Dollar-a-Day.
